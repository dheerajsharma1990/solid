# 4.35 — Travel Guide: Travel Food & Snacks (Cook with Shrreya)

## Travel tips
- **Take the child out** — after the 40-day rituals, start going out (for 6 months on exclusive feed; after 6 months travel by **car, flight, train, or two-wheeler** — all allowed). Staying indoors doesn't prevent illness.
- **Carry food matching your travel hours:** 2-hr trip → a 2-hr item; airport + 5-hr flight → an 8-hr item; 10-hr train → a 10-hr item. **Always keep a backup in your purse**, even if you'll buy food outside.
- **Backup categories:**
  1. **Dry snacks (airtight, 7–8 days):** laddu (makhana/nachni/rice/wheat-dry-fruit), moong/fenugreek/coriander/masala puri, nacho-triangle crispy puris.
  2. **Dry timepass:** makhana, churmura, lahi, jowar/wheat puffs — **roast at home in ghee + a pinch of black salt**; don't give market versions as-is.
  3. **Market buys:** Rajgira/amaranth laddu, chikki (don't spoil).
  4. **Fresh:** a **banana case** (Amazon ~₹80, keeps banana unbruised/unblackened); an **airtight container with a spoon** carried empty (add a fresh idli or hot khichdi from a hotel en route); a **comfort food** (small bread pieces, cheese cube/slice, salted paneer).
  5. **Instant mix:** **NO market Cerelac** (sugar-added, flavored). Use **homemade Cerelac** or MyDvija **Satvic mix / Ceramics**; or soak+roast+powder moong dal & rice; or the churmura–futana-dal powder shown in this section — add hot water, cover, ready.
- **Drop the cereal-protein-fiber pressure during travel** — mom's focus shouldn't be food. Banana, laddu, idli — whatever works. Hotels usually have **sauteed paneer, steamed french beans/carrots, fresh dal-khichdi.**
- **Teething food on travel:** neem teether, carrot/radish stick, fresh-fruit teether (net type).
- **Seating options:** car-seat **lower seat belt**, a hotel high chair, a parent's lap, or a sofa corner.
- **What to carry:** airtight container + spoon, a **big bib with a tray/compartment**, a **thermos/bottle for hot water** (+ formula), **extra water** (for washing), a **small bedsheet/mat**, soft napkins + wet wipes, and **coconut oil/moisturizer** (repeated face-washing causes dry-skin rashes). For flights, a **microwave-proof container**; for trains, a thermos. A **travel cooker** (e.g., Nova, with a coil heater + spoon) is a must.
- **Breastfeed more during travel;** feed in the car; teach the child to watch out the train window. For activities: YouTube → **"Travel Activities Mahiduja."**
- **If the child won't eat while travelling:** increase formula/breastmilk, resume solids on return. Eating off your plate (a little salt/spice) is fine; if the child eats only curd/fruits, that's still normal food; for cold/cough, give soup.

## The recipes
1. **Rice karkure.** Rice flour (or jowar) + a little besan/moong-dal flour + a pinch of baking soda (1 yr+); skip chilli for small kids. Mix with ½ cup water into a **thin, lump-free paste**, cook/steam while stirring until thick and **transparent in color** *(color change shown at* **[00:12:57]***)*, cool, then **deep-fry** (deep-frying is fine at home with **fresh, non-reused oil**). Sprinkle mild/pink/rock salt (1 yr) or a little red chilli (11 mo+).
2. **Chole balls** (high-protein). Chole soaked overnight + ½ cup jaggery powder + dry-fruit powder (**almonds + walnuts compulsory**, dates optional). Cook chole (**3–5 whistles**), cool, drain, blend with jaggery (**no water**), add dry-fruit powder, set in fridge **2 hrs**, mold into balls.
3. **Caramelized makhana** (lasts 3–4 days). Big bowl makhana + 2 spoons ghee + ½ bowl jaggery powder + a little salt/chilli (skip for small). Roast makhana crispy; on **low flame** melt jaggery in ghee (add more ghee if slow), mix in makhana, cool.
4. **Besan coconut burfi.** Besan (**moong-dal flour if <1 yr**) + ½ bowl ghee + desiccated coconut + ⅓ bowl jaggery powder. Roast flour until aromatic, add ghee to a flowy paste, add jaggery, spread on butter paper / greased plate, garnish with coconut + dry fruits. (In-laws' version uses wheat flour.)
5. **Dalia cake.** 1 bowl plain broken wheat/dalia (**not MyDvija dalia — it has added ingredients**; use proper plain dalia) + baking powder + baking soda + curd + milk + jaggery + oil. Powder the dalia; mix curd + oil + jaggery + milk, add dalia powder, rest **15 min**, then add baking soda/powder + milk for consistency; pour into a mold dusted with dry-fruit powder; bake in pan/cooker/oven ~5 min (test with a stick).
6. **Nutrient-rich makhana balls** (lasts 8–10 days; doubles as dessert). Big bowl makhana + 3 spoons dry-fruit powder + **homemade peanut butter** + ½ bowl dry-dates powder + cardamom (**possible allergen — be careful**). Roast makhana crispy, powder, mix in jaggery powder + peanut butter + dry-fruit powder + cardamom; roll small balls. (Can melt jaggery instead of using powder.)
7. **Primix (instant travel food).** 1 bowl puffed rice/churmura + dry fruits (almonds, pistachios) + roasted futana/chana dal (daliya). Roast all, blend to powder, store. To serve: powder + **hot boiling water** (lump-free), add mashed banana/fruit.
8. **Makhana chivda** (lasts 8–10 days). Makhana + roasted groundnuts + cut almonds + ghee + curry leaves + turmeric (+ coconut, cashews, peanuts only **11 mo+**; for an 11-month baby use **only makhana** — *the toothless-friendly version is shown ready at* **[00:26:55]**). Roast everything (less water = better); a pinch of black salt for 11 mo+.
9. **Chocolate peanut-butter balls** (good filler; fridge airtight 6–7 days). 1 tsp cocoa powder + 2 tsp homemade peanut butter + other ingredients (sprinkles optional — skip for very small); blend, roll into balls. (Homemade peanut-butter recipe promised in the bonus section.)
10. **Fruit popsicles** (teething solution, summer) *(shown at* **[00:30:14]***)*. Seasonal fruit puree (mango, watermelon, orange, kiwi, muskmelon) + water; pour into a **popsicle mould**, freeze overnight or 5–6 hrs (don't keep checking).
11. **Masala groundnuts** (lasts 2–3 days; **not for 11-month babies**). Groundnuts + gram flour + 2 spoons rice flour + red chilli + salt. Mix gram flour + salt + spices, coat peanuts, **add water little by little** (gram flour clumps if water is added suddenly), then **deep-fry** bhajiya-style. Also a hit with spicy-food-loving family members.
12. **Moong puri** (lasts 10 days) *(kneading & rolling shown at* **[00:34:10]–[00:34:50]***)*. Moong dal (washed, soaked **1+ hr**; can grind a little but a granular texture is nice) + rice flour + cumin + ginger-chilli paste (**remove chilli <11 mo, keep ginger**) + ghee + coriander + salt (1 yr+) or a pinch of black salt (<11 mo). Knead a dough that's **not too wet or dry** (the white color comes from rice flour); add dry fenugreek for taste; shape small puris by hand or rolling pin; deep-fry golden. (Home deep-frying with fresh oil is fine, unlike market frying.)
13. **Carrot balls** (dessert/travel; make fresh — lasts only 1–2 days, ~1.5–3 days refrigerated). Grated carrot + dates + almonds + oats + cardamom + essence (optional) + desiccated coconut; blend to a paste, roll, coat in desiccated coconut. (Roasting the carrot in ghee first extends shelf life but takes longer.)
