# 4.31 — Breakfast Recipes (Cook with Shrreya)

## 5 rules of breakfast
1. **Don't let breakfast slip** because the child woke at 10–11 — the child should wake by **8–8:30**; breakfast is a key meal, never skip it.
2. Breakfast must keep the **cereal + protein + fiber** combination (not just fruit, or coconut water, or biscuits, or plain chapati).
3. Breakfast improves the child's **cognition** (memory, learning, good/bad decision-making) → cognitive development.
4. It's the **kick-start** for an energetic, playful day.
5. On-time breakfast → child does the day's activities well and **sleeps well at night**.

*All recipes below are references — change the flour, vegetables, flavors, and tempering freely.*

## The recipes
1. **Ragi millet idli (no soda, no eno).** Soak 1 cup ragi + ½ cup idli rice + ½ cup urad dal + whole fenugreek seeds; wash 2–4 times, soak **4–6 hrs**, grind to thick paste with **¼ cup poha (soaked 10 min)** to idli-batter consistency; ferment overnight or 5–6 hrs; steam. **Salt only if 1 yr+.** Can also be made with rice flour. Served with chutney.
2. **Fruit pancake / chilla (e.g., mango chilla).** Any fruit puree + jaggery + a little milk (**soy/almond <1 yr; animal milk >1 yr**) + water → chilla/dosa consistency; cook in ghee. Make **coin-size small pancakes** for fussy eaters (they eat better).
3. **Makhana kheer (high protein).** Roast makhana in ghee (for **6–8 months**, **half-crush** it so it's not lumpy in the mouth); add milk (**almond/oat/soy <1 yr; animal >1 yr**), 1 tsp **dates powder**, dry-fruit powder, jaggery, a little **saffron** (powder it for <1 yr). Roasting in ghee brings out flavor and avoids sogginess.
4. **Vegetable waffle.** Moong-dal flour (+ a little rice flour) + favorite vegetables + lots of coriander + **ajwain** + **buttermilk** (compulsory; adds pre/probiotic) + salt if 1 yr+. Keep the batter **thick** (not liquid) for the mini waffle machine (Amazon).
5. **Makhana–carrot puree** *(visual: "look at the taste" at* **[00:12:33]***).* 1 bowl makhana (roasted in ghee) + 1 bowl grated carrot, blended (stays a bit liquid as carrot releases water), then cooked in a pan with a glass of water ~**5 min**; finish with **1 tbsp cow ghee**, cumin powder, a little jaggery; cook 5 min more. **From 6 months;** good for a **sick child / cold-cough** (give as evening snack or a full meal for the very young). Can swap **red pumpkin** for carrot. *(Never give plain carrot puree — add protein.)*
6. **Moong-dal chilla (besan-style).** Use **moong-dal flour instead of besan** + grated carrot, coriander, onion, **ginger-garlic paste** (extra important when not adding salt), **buttermilk**, salt if 1 yr+. *You can use MyDvija multigrain mix flour directly here (* **[00:13:30]** *); the finished idli-batter-consistency paste is shown at* **[00:14:15]**. Cook **crispy** in oil/ghee, cut into shapes, serve with curd or chutney.
7. **Poha–banana mash (instant, travel-friendly).** Roast banana in ghee (tastes great, doesn't turn bitter), smash; add **poha soaked 10 min** + water; mash with a pav-bhaji smasher → puree form. **From 5th month.** Add makhana/dry-fruit powder.
8. **Instant rava/semolina upma mix (travel).** Temper jeera powder + fenugreek seeds + **urad dal** (allowed from 6 months) until color changes; add **curry leaves** (finely chopped/blended for the very young; good for the liver); roast **1.5 cups thick rava**; salt + green chillies only if 1 yr+; 2 drops lemon. **Store** the dry mix; to serve, add boiled water, cover **10 min**.
9. **Whole moong / sprouts waffle.** 2 cups moong soaked overnight (sprouts even better) + **oats flour** (or jowar/rice flour) + ginger-garlic paste + chopped onion + grated carrot + lots of coriander; chilli only if 1 yr+. Keep batter **thick**; mini waffle machine; cut into sticks/shapes.
10. **Sevai upma (instant, travel).** Rice-flour sevai (or rava/jowar). Temper mustard seeds + cumin powder + asafoetida + urad dal + curry leaves (chopped for young) + cashew/cashew-powder + (groundnut powder if child <1.5 yr and not yet an independent eater) + semolina; roast to orange/brown; salt if 1 yr+. **Stores 8–10 days.** To serve, add boiling water, cover **10 min**.
11. **Banana–oats waffle/pancake.** MyDvija oats powder (or homemade) + banana (or **sweet potato**) + ½ cup milk (animal only >1 yr); add dry-fruit powder.
12. **Red-pumpkin soup / smoothie.** Ghee + cumin + garlic + curry leaves + onion + red pumpkin (carrot or sweet potato also complement); saute in a small cooker, add water, **4–5 whistles**, remove curry leaves, smash with pav-bhaji smasher (no need for a paste), top with black pepper; salt if 1 yr+. Soup for older kids, smoothie for younger.

## Closing tip
Make the food **so tasty you'd want to eat it yourself** — if you wouldn't, the child won't. If a dish isn't working, **renew its taste, texture, shape, size, or serving style.**
