# Section 4 — Cooking Time: "Cook with Shrreya" (Overview)

*Source video: "What's in it — Cooking time, let's be master chef for little ones"*

This is the hands-on recipe-demonstration section ("Cook with Shrreya"), with **40–60+ recipes** (continually being expanded):
- **Breakfast** (10–12 ideas), **Lunch** (10–12), **Dinner** (10–12), **Travel snacks** (10–12), **Soups & drinks** (8–10), plus **homemade staples** (cheese/dahi/mayo/ketchup/paneer/spreads) and **healing/recovery food**.

## How to use this section
- **All recipes are samples/references, not a fixed menu.** Swap freely: any leafy vegetable for another (spinach ↔ fenugreek), any dal for another, any flour for another. You get a **base idea** and many combinations.
- **Don't copy menus from Instagram/YouTube/Google.** Plan a menu suited to **your family's culture** (an Andhra family's fish-curry-rice ≠ a Maharashtrian roti-sabzi-dal-rice ≠ a North-Indian paratha lunch). A **sample menu is in the notes/index** section.
- The instructor asks viewers to cook, photograph, and share in the group whether the kids liked it.

> **Note on timestamps:** These are cooking demos, so techniques are shown visually. Each recipe summary flags the most useful "look at this / I'll show you" moments with their timestamps so you can jump to the technique in the video.
