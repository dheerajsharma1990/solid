# 4.34 — Soups & Juices (Cook with Shrreya)

## General rules for soups & juices
- **Serve at 5:30 pm, not after 6 pm** (later → children sweat a lot).
- Most **juices here are summer coolers** — use in summer / when the child is sweating.
- **Basic soup spices:** pepper, coriander, black salt. For more taste: a **garlic tempering**, or saute the vegetables in **ghee + cumin**.
- **Consistency:** as a **snack** = medium idli-batter consistency; as a **meal/dinner** = **thick**.
- **Never use a hand mixer** — use a **pav-bhaji smasher** (keeps a granular texture, which the child should get used to; fully blending the fiber removes the soup's natural "cooling" effect).
- **Soup is a snack, not a stomach-filler** — kids have small appetites, so soup-then-food rarely both fit.
- **Mom's tip:** add a powder of **dry mint / dry curry leaves / dry coriander** to the soup for taste.
- Soups help with **constipation, cough**, and developing **taste buds**; great as sick-child food.

## The recipes
1. **Moong-dal & milk soup** (+ rice = **malagatani**). Moong dal + milk + bottle gourd + ginger. Saute bottle gourd in ghee in the cooker, add moong dal (+ rice for malagatani), cook. **High-protein dinner**; non-veg families can cook the dal in **chicken stock**. Alternatives: masoor or green moong dal (+ carrot). Smash with pav-bhaji smasher; **thick for dinner, thinner (more water) for a snack.**
2. **Red pumpkin soup** — good for **vitamin A, eyesight, growth, immunity, dehydration, loose motions, fever**. Chopped pumpkin + carrot + onion (optional) + ghee + cumin + curry leaves + garlic, sauteed in cooker, **4–5 whistles**, smashed *(shown at* **[00:07:14]***)*. A **thick puree** version works from **6 months.**
3. **Drumstick / Moringa soup** (superfood, very nutrient-rich). Drumstick (or moringa leaves) + tomato + onion + ginger + garlic (+ masoor dal for taste) + turmeric + cumin + black pepper; saute a tempering first, cook, then **grind and STRAIN** *(straining/removing the skin shown at* **[00:08:54]** *and* **[00:09:27]***)* — drumstick has lots of fiber/skin that looks odd in soup. Adjust consistency, add cumin + black pepper + salt (1 yr+); **½ cup cold milk** improves color; finish with 2 drops lemon.
4. **Oats–chiku milkshake** (high-calorie). Cook oats 2–5 min, **cool**, blend with cooked chiku + almonds (+ optional dates) + milk (**formula/expressed breastmilk <1 yr; cow's milk 11 mo+**).
5. **Ragi lassi / Ambil** (traditional Maharashtrian). Cook **ragi sattva** in ½ glass water (optionally roast in ghee first), add **jaggery (limited)**, blend with **½ cup beaten homemade curd** + cardamom powder + water to adjust. Whole family. **Non-sweet version:** skip jaggery, use **buttermilk + garlic tempering** instead.
6. **Grape juice** (summer cooler). Whole grapes are a **choking hazard**, so juicing is the safe way to give this seasonal fruit. Blend grapes + black pepper + black salt + cardamom powder (**skip black salt & cardamom before 8 months**) + chilled/matka water + 2 mint leaves; squeeze **lemon** on top for vitamin C.
7. **Mint & jaggery syrup** (summer cooler, whole family). **Sabja/tulsi (vegetable) seeds** (soaked) + chaat masala + salt (skip before 8 months; little after) + black pepper + **mint puree** + **jaggery water** (soak jaggery 2–4 hrs) + lemon. Keeps some fiber (don't over-blend).
8. **Kokum + coconut milk (sol-kadhi style).** Wash kokum, soak **30 min** (the pink is natural — no artificial color). Make **coconut milk** (blend coconut, add water, squeeze — *white milk comes out, shown at* **[00:16:00]**). Mix kokum into the coconut milk + a little salt; coriander optional (skip to keep it pink / for small kids). Short season but a must-try; whole family.
9. **Raw mango panha / aam panna** (seasonal). 3 raw mangoes — remove skin & pulp (let it cool first), mix with **jaggery** (balances sourness) into a pulpy liquid, **strain**. Store in fridge **2–4 days**; serve with water / ice. All age groups.

## Salt note
Don't use black/pink/Himalayan salt for small children at all (a **half pinch** only if the child eats nothing). After 1 year (when normal salt is allowed), a little pink/black salt makes soups tasty. **All of these are family foods — make them for the whole family.**
