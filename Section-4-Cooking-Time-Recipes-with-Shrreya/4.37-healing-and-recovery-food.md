## Why children fall sick & the recovery cycle
- A child's **immunity is still developing**, so infections are caught easily.
- Per doctors, the child is "well" in **5–6 days**, but **full recovery** (normal eating, sleep) takes **2–3 weeks**. If a new infection hits within those weeks, the 3-week cycle restarts — the "always sick" loop.

## 5 challenges when a child is sick
1. **Digestion is compromised** — colic, vomiting, loose motions, or the body blocks food ("not feeling like eating" = the body saying *let it heal first*).
2. **Taste buds are compromised** — the child suddenly eats only one thing (e.g., dalia) or rejects it.
3. **Food rejection** (favorite and non-favorite) is common.
4. **Parents' mistake:** force-feeding or feeding with the TV on — this builds a bad habit you'll spend 2–3 weeks correcting; 2 bites that way isn't worth it.
5. **Wrong food choices** — biscuits, sweets, high-sugar/processed/maida foods **delay healing.**

## The approach
- **No force feeding.**
- Give **immunity- + taste-bud- + digestive-fire (jatharagni)-improving foods intermittently** — hence the heavy use of **black pepper, ghee, and tempering** in these recipes.
- **No choices** — stick to routine; sit the child down, offer, and accept whether they eat or not.
- A **300–500 g** weight drop over 2 weeks is normal; regaining it takes **2–4 months** — don't stress (the child looking "dry" is normal).
- **If the child is non-active / has fever / rejects food → doctor's medicines** (no home remedies). **If playful, active, no fever → home remedies are fine.**
- **Recovery weeks:** week 1 — minimal food; week 2 — 2–3 bites; end of week 3 — normal quantity (whatever feels normal). **No target amount** — offer 2–3 bites, wait ~30 min, offer again (installments).
- **Focus ingredients:** gooseberry (amla), ragi, rajgira/amaranth flour, rice flour, chicken stock, quinoa/jowar dalia → protein, vitamin C, multivitamin, immunity.

## The recipes
1. **Malagatani soup (healing).** Softly-cooked rice (smash extra if the child can't bite). Temper pepper + ginger-garlic + onion (jain: skip onion) in the cooker; add **red pumpkin + apple (with peel — important)** + washed lentils (**avoid toor & chana**; masoor/moong/black-urad fine); **3–4 whistles**; blend (naturally thick) and **strain** out the coarse fiber; turmeric; salt or black salt by age; 2 drops lemon. Serve plain, or with the soft rice — great for protein/strength/recovery.
2. **Gooseberry (amla) soup / rasam.** 5–6 raw gooseberries + tomato + curry leaves + coriander + mustard + cumin + garlic + pepper + turmeric. Make a thick paste of pepper + cumin + garlic, crush in the raw gooseberry; in a pan: ghee/oil + cumin + curry leaves + tomato; add the ground mix + water, cook quickly; turmeric; salt 1 yr+. Gut healing + taste buds + vitamin C.
3. **Bajra por (bajra wrap / porridge).** Bajra flour (or rajgira/ragi/rice/jowar) + jaggery + 2 spoons ghee + black pepper + **dry-ginger (saunth) powder** + ajwain. Cook the flour separately, **stirring constantly to avoid lumps**, then add a tempering of ghee + cumin + ajwain + pepper. Improves taste buds, digestion, strength.
4. **Red pumpkin soup (healing, easy to digest).** ½ bowl chopped red pumpkin + garlic + curry leaves + whole cumin + ghee. Saute, add pumpkin, cooker, remove curry leaves, **smash** (no hand-mixer), black pepper, salt/black salt by age, 2 drops lemon for young children. *(Same method → **sweet potato soup**.)*
5. **Almond–broccoli soup ("Flu Soup").** For a sore throat / not eating. Ajwain + black pepper + soaked-peeled almonds + onion (optional) + garlic + broccoli. Ghee tempering + ajwain + whole pepper, cook garlic, add broccoli, salt/black salt, water, cooker **2–4 whistles**, blend.
6. **Banana–curd–jaggery (teething relief).** ½ cup yogurt + jaggery + dry-fruit powder + 2 bananas (not raw, not overripe). Smash banana, mix in curd; also slice banana **thick** and dip; **refrigerate** — the cold soothes teething. Also a weight gainer.
7. **Sago (sabudana)–pomegranate kheer.** For **loose motions / poor digestion**. Soak 2 spoons sago ~1 hr, boil in 2–3 cups water until **transparent** (sago is starch → a **binder**; **do NOT give if constipated**); top with dry-fruit powder. Use only **2–4 days**, not daily.
8. **Ragi ambil (buttermilk-based, rich calcium).** Sprouted ragi flour (nachni sattva) + cumin powder + coriander (optional) + crushed garlic + 1 bowl buttermilk. Make a **lump-free ragi slurry** (add to slightly-boiled water **little by little**), cook; add garlic + cumin + salt; ghee-garlic tempering (half a chilli for adults). **Add buttermilk only AFTER cooking** (never cook buttermilk). Serve **warm, not cold.**
9. **Quinoa / jowar khichdi (recovery, when strength is low).** Quinoa (or jowar khichdi, or MyDvija dalia) washed 2–3×, soaked 10–30 min; cook the quinoa in water. Temper ghee + groundnuts (**powder for small kids**) + mustard + urad dal + onion + tomato + peas (strong-aroma veg first, soft veg later); add the cooked quinoa; pinch Kashmiri red chilli + salt 1 yr+; garnish coriander + **lemon (improves iron absorption)**. High protein.
10. **Lajamanda (ancient recipe).** 1 cup popped rice/lahi + 14 cups water; boil on high then medium, cook, **filter**, give the **liquid** to the child. For loose motions / recovery.
11. **Chicken stew / stock.** Chicken bones + water + black pepper + turmeric. High protein, immunity. For non-veg families (the instructor is vegetarian, so she doesn't cook it on-camera but recommends it).
