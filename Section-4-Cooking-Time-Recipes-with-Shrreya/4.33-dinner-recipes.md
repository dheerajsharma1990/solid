# 4.33 — Dinner Recipes (Cook with Shrreya)

## Rules of dinner
- **Timing:** start **7:00–7:15**, finish by **7:30–7:45.**
- **Make it high-protein:** cheese, milk, curd, paneer, lentils, sprouts, chicken, egg/egg-white/omelet.
- **Bedtime top-up** (child sleeps ~11 pm): 1 cup **turmeric milk or ghee milk** (**cow's milk**) to enhance sleep.
- **Avoid anything that stimulates the brain / raises blood sugar at night** — chocolate, cocoa, desserts, sugar — they activate brain function and harm sleep. Also avoid gas/colic-causing foods.
- **Reuse lunch/breakfast items:** parathas, thepla, pancake, waffle, sauteed idli, dal-roti, dal-rice, masala/veg khichdi + raita all work for dinner.
- **Find the hunger time:** if the child eats the **6–6:30 snack** heavily and then isn't interested in dinner, give an **early dinner (6 pm)** + a small **high-protein item (paneer/cheese cube, chicken piece, egg white) at 7–8 pm.**
- **Mistakes to avoid:** don't give a bowl of sweet kheer at 11:30 pm just to make the child sleep. After the child is **8 kg+**, night waking is **not hunger** — it's oral stimulation / soothing / habit / association — so don't keep feeding milk; don't stress.

## The recipes
1. **Beetroot-carrot pasta (red).** Whole-wheat or millet pasta + cashews + beetroot + carrot (use **spinach** for green). Boil water + ghee/oil + salt (1 yr+); **doneness test:** lift one piece on a spoon and see if a fork **cuts** it easily; **remove from water** when done. Sauce: saute beetroot + carrot + cashew in ghee (in cooker), add water, **3–4 whistles**, blend → veg-based sauce. *(Cashew is a possible allergen — swap **grated paneer**, sauteed not pressure-cooked.)* Toss pasta in; top with dates/dry-fruit powder. *(Color shown at* **[00:07:37]** *— "look at the colour.")*
2. **Makhana tikki.** Boiled potato (or **cooked raw banana** — improves gut) + **roasted makhana powder** (roast for crunch, then powder) + coriander + grated carrot + cumin powder (+ chilli/salt only if not small; + optional ginger-garlic + lemon; + grated red pumpkin/bottle gourd). Shape (use **moulds** for fun shapes), crisp both sides in ghee/oil ~**3–4 min/side** until golden (add a little rice for extra crunch). High protein + calcium, good for bones. Even if the child won't eat the tikki, they'll eat the sabzi + makhana.
3. **Ragi/nachni noodles with cauliflower-cashew "white sauce."** Ragi/millet noodles + spice masala + **cauliflower + cashew gravy** (tastes like white sauce — **no cheese, cornflour, or rice flour**). **Trick:** cook noodles separately, then saute ghee + gravy + water + the cooked noodles (cooking noodles *in* the gravy takes too long). Or maggi-style: boil water, add masala, saute veg, cook noodles in the same water. Finish: coriander + dry fenugreek or oregano.
4. **Moringa rice** (superfood — amino acids, anti-inflammatory, immunity, strength). Mixed lentils (**avoid toor & chana dal <1 yr**; urad/moong/masoor fine) + rice + **moringa-leaf paste** (or moringa powder) + green peas (or grated carrot) + finely chopped onion; ghee + cumin tempering in cooker.
5. **Banana–moong-dal pancake.** Soaked moong dal (or sprouted) + 1.5 cup banana + milk (or almond milk) + dry-fruit powder. The **dal makes it a proper (heavy-protein) dinner** → great satiety; reduce the dal for an early dinner. Use **ghee smartly** (good fat → satiety) but keep dinner by 7:30. Cook **small pancakes on a slow flame**, both sides. Sweet; liked by kids and adults.
6. **Moringa noodles** (the instructor's younger daughter's favorite — goes in the school tiffin). MyDvija moringa noodles; the sachet (onion powder, chilli, salt) only for **1 yr+** — for younger use coriander + cumin powder + oregano + black pepper. Cook separately or maggi-style with onion + tomato + masala; adding water makes a **gravy** kids love. **From 6 months.** *(Veg version shown at* **[00:18:08]***; a friend's son eating it shown at* **[00:18:16]***.)*
7. **Handwa (gut-healing, fermented).** Soak flour in **curd 4–5 hrs** (prebiotic + probiotic). Add sesame seeds, mustard seeds, finely chopped onion, ginger-garlic paste, cabbage, capsicum, cauliflower, grated carrot, broccoli, curry leaves; **2 pinches soda or eno** (after the 4–6 hr ferment). Keep the **paste thick**. Cook in a pan (non-stick, or a non-aluminium pan — don't cook kids' food in aluminium) with mustard/sesame/curry-leaf/turmeric/cumin tempering, **5–10 min**. For small kids, make **balls in an appam pan.**
8. **Millet pasta with carrot–onion–tomato tangy gravy.** Millet pasta; **add crushed/sauteed paneer** to make it a complete meal; salt 1 yr+. Cook pasta (fork test; **remove from water immediately** or it keeps cooking). Use **oil (not ghee) for pasta**; saute onion + tomato (+ optional ginger-garlic) + boiled carrot + paneer (gives a "khoya" effect, complements carrot) + optional cashew; black pepper, oregano, herbs, MyDvija masala (1 yr+). Toss pasta; garnish coriander/kasuri methi. The veg-blended gravy means no veggies to pick out.
9. **Egg-spinach-oats paratha/chilla** (weight gainer; iron, fiber, brain booster, reduces constipation). ½ cup oats powder + 5–6 spinach leaves + **egg yolk** (or paneer / grated cheese on top for eggless) + black pepper + **flaxseed powder (superfood)** + salt (avoid for small). Blend spinach + egg, add spices + oats powder; cook on a non-stick pan (intact coating), **cover to cook the egg through**, flip.
10. **"Special case" white pasta** (a treat, cooked on-camera with daughter Sachi). Wheat spiral pasta + **wheat flour** (healthier roux) + butter + grated cheese + **cold milk** + salt + chilli flakes + oregano + **ginger-garlic paste + Maggi masala** (the "two magic ingredients"). Boil pasta in salted, oiled water 10–15 min. Gravy: melt butter → ginger-garlic (brown) → 2 tsp wheat flour (stir immediately until brown) → water (stir continuously, no clots) → cold milk → cheese → oregano + chilli flakes + Maggi masala. Toss pasta. *(An indulgent cheese-heavy version; more veg can be added to make it healthier.)*
