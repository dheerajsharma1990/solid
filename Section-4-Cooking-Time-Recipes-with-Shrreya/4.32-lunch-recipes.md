# 4.32 — Lunch Recipes (Cook with Shrreya)

## 10 rules of lunch
1. **Timing 12–2 pm.**
2. **Not a "timepass" item** — set a patience limit (the instructor's is **~25 min**); after your limit (say 25–30 min) the plate goes back to the kitchen. If the child ate little, **offer a snack 30–45 min later.**
3. Playing with / touching / mouthing food is **normal**. **No force feeding, no distraction, no screen** *(the screen-distraction mistake is called out at* **[00:01:20]** *as what NOT to do)* — these make the child eat even less.
4. **Cereal + protein + fiber** is important (lunch is a key meal).
5. **Naps:** an 8-month-old usually has one pre-lunch and one post-lunch nap. **Don't feed when very sleepy or over-hungry** — feed when *happily hungry.*
6. If the child ate well, **involve them in cooking.**
7. "Eats puree well" does **not** mean give only puree.
8. **Comfort food needs a companion** — e.g., a sweet-potato dish + dal-rice balls + paneer toss = a complete meal.
9. **Water with lunch is fine**, but not a lot before/with/after.
10. **Tooth-care tip of the day:** put a **cucumber or carrot stick** in the child's hand during lunch — natural teeth cleaning. *(Recipe list is in the index —* **[00:03:50]***.)*

## The recipes
1. **Sweet-potato slices.** Thin slices roasted in **cow ghee** (cover, ~10–15 min), then stir in **jaggery + dry-fruit powder**. Good for brain, weight, calories; science suggests **sweet potato from 8 months–1 year.** Serve with dal-rice balls / paneer for a full lunch.
2. **Chatpata paneer** (also great for dinner / as a side for plain-chapati eaters). Saute **homemade chopped paneer** golden in ghee (to slice homemade paneer: strain, rinse, press under a weight, cool). Separately: ghee/oil + ginger-garlic + onion (skip if colic) sauteed brown + **cooked** tomato puree (not sour) + jaggery + sesame seeds + capsicum/carrot/red pumpkin + **tulsi leaves**; keep it **water-based** (no soy sauce); add the paneer. Salt 1 yr+ (or from 7 months if not avoiding). Colorful — "Holi in a plate."
3. **Multi-millet noodles** (jowar/bajra/kodo/proso; **no maida**, fertilizer-free → cook longer). Boil water, add noodles + pinch salt (1 yr+) or ghee, cook (noodles ~double in size, **don't make soggy**), strain. Saute garlic + vegetables (finely chopped <7 mo, grated 7–7.5 mo) in oil/ghee (**from 6 months**) with turmeric/coriander/cumin (use the noodle **spice mix only 1 yr+**); add noodles, **mix gently** (don't break them), **steam 2 min** so flavors meld.
4. **Potato-rice (high-calorie weight-gainer).** Pure calories — **only** for kids needing weight gain / not eating; **never at dinner**, not 2–3×/week. Soak rice, cook in cooker with coriander + potato. **Balanced variant** = add moong dal/lentils + carrots → **mix-veg khichdi** (this *can* go 2–3×/week). Smash thick; add moong dal → **tikkis**; for puree-eaters it's a good paste with ghee-cumin tempering.
5. **Rainbow paneer-veggie pulao** (quick office-day lunch; +raita = dinner). 1 cup cooked rice + mixed veg (grated/chopped) + **1 bowl grated paneer (in place of dal)** + sesame seeds. Oil/ghee + ginger-garlic + onion (skip if colic), saute; **black pepper** (allowed from 6 months) + cumin powder; add grated paneer, then rice (keep **mushy**, not overcooked); form balls, garnish with seeds. ~10–15 min; leftover rice → tadka rice.
6. **Quinoa pulao/khichdi.** Green peas + tomato + **urad dal (from 6 months)** + groundnuts (**powdered/half-crushed** for small kids). Wash quinoa 4–5× like rice, soak 10–45 min, cook in **1.5 bowl water ~15–20 min** (or cooker). Temper groundnuts + mustard + urad dal + veg; add quinoa + water (khichdi or pulao style); turmeric/cumin/coriander; salt 1 yr+; finish with coriander, ghee, lemon. Quinoa = rich fiber + protein.
7. **Paneer kathiawadi roll** (swap paneer for **mushroom/tofu/soy chunks/soaked lentils** as filling). Marinate paneer in **curd + basic spices + ginger-garlic + oil** (+ salt 1 yr+, + saunf powder; can omit ginger-garlic/onion for a jain version). Knead dough, roll a big roti, cook **half-done**; cook the marinated paneer (drain water so the roti doesn't go soggy); assemble with a cheese / tomato-puree base + paneer + favorite veg, roll, and crisp.
8. **Palak paneer paratha.** Wheat flour + grated paneer + coriander + onion (optional) + cumin + ginger/garlic (green chilli avoid <11 mo). **Blanch spinach** (steam, then plunge in **ice water** to keep the color — **no soda**), puree with garlic; salt 1 yr+. **Two methods:** stuffed paratha, or knead the **palak puree + grated paneer into the dough**. Sprinkle sesame seeds (nice white dots). Iron + calcium; serve with curd, cut into fun shapes.
9. **Khichdi / dal-chawal tikki (cutlet).** 1.5 cup khichdi mix (dal-chawal powder) — or cooked dal-chawal, or MyDvija khichdi mix — + 1.5 cup chopped cabbage + smashed (boiled) potato + **flaxseed powder (superfood)** + coriander + lemon + grated carrot/red pumpkin (+ optional ginger-garlic). Mix (wheat + a little rice flour ok); shape into coins/sticks; crisp both sides. Great for lunch **and** dinner.
10. **Multivitamin curd rice** (immunity booster; good for kids who "don't eat curd"). 1 bowl cooked rice + ½ cup curd + grated beetroot + grated carrot + grated cucumber + coriander + **groundnut POWDER** (never whole — choking risk) + tempering of mustard seeds + chopped curry leaves. Raw veg is fine unless the child has heavy colic (then steam first).

## Closing planning tip
Plan a **2-week menu** (Mon–Sun, then repeat) — that's enough; you don't need new dishes daily. (The section gives **8–10 weeks** of ideas across all meals.) Make **prep-heavy recipes on weekends.** Share the child's eating photos in the group.
