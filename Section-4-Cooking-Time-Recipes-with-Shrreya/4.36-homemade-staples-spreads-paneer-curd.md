# 4.36 — Homemade Staples: Cheese, Curd, Mayo, Ketchup, Paneer, Peanut Spread, Hummus, Chutney

**Why make these at home:** family members push store-bought paneer/curd/mayo/ketchup/chyawanprash/Nutella. If you buy, **read the ingredients** — avoid **palm oil**, and watch for **sugar as the 2nd/3rd ingredient**. If you must give a store item, at most **once in 15 days**; for routine use, **make it at home.** Try each once, then decide what's easy enough to keep making.

## Powders & chutneys
1. **Gunpowder (idli podi).** Chana dal (**moong dal if <1 yr**) + sesame seeds + asafoetida (+ a small seed of chilli for 1 yr) + salt (1 yr+). **Dry-roast** the dals 2–3 min on high flame, stirring, until brownish and fragrant; cool; blend to a dry powder. High iron + protein. Great with hot rice + ghee, or on idli.
2. **Groundnut chutney** (wet; fridge airtight 1–2 days). Chana dal (use **phutana / roasted Bengal-gram dal** for small kids — no roasting/soaking needed) + groundnut + curd + grated coconut + urad dal + mustard + ginger + garlic + curry leaves. Soak dals 10–15 min; roast ginger/garlic/groundnut/coconut/curry leaves in ghee; blend with the soaked dals + curd + a little water; finish with a **mustard-seed tempering**. Salt 1 yr+. With paratha/idli/uttapam/cutlet/tikki.
3. **Moringa chutney** (wet; fridge 1–2 days). Moringa leaves (or powder) + fresh coconut + 3 spoons sesame + urad dal + cumin + garlic. Roast dal + sesame in oil until brownish/fragrant, add washed-and-dried moringa leaves, roast, blend. Calcium-rich.

## Spreads
4. **Mayonnaise** (fridge ~2 days). 100 g fresh smashed homemade paneer (cow's-milk) + 12–15 cashews (soaked 5–6 hrs) + lemon + garlic + 1 spoon black pepper; blend with water to consistency. Spread on paratha/khakhra.
5. **Chocolate spread (Nutella-style)** (fridge 4–6 days). Almonds + dates + cocoa powder + milk powder + chocolate + melted chocolate + milk; blend. Can also make an ice-cream roll.
6. **Hummus** (a "foreign chutney," high protein). Overnight-soaked-and-cooked chickpeas + ½ bowl roasted sesame + cumin + garlic + oil + salt + lemon; blend with water. Give **within a year if no severe colic**; serve with sticks/paratha/khakhra.
7. **Peanut butter** (**1 yr+**; fridge 4–6 days). Only groundnuts: roast 10–15 min, peel, blend in **pulse mode** 2–3 times to a wet paste, then run continuously until sticky/done. Optional date syrup/jaggery for a sweet version.

## Homemade cheese, ketchup, curd & paneer (live demos)
8. **Homemade "eno cheese" / spicy paneer spread.** Curdle milk with lemon → strain the paneer (**save the whey for soup**), squeeze. Mix **eno (~1 small spoon per litre) + water (whisk to a foam)** with the curdled paneer + turmeric (no salt <1 yr); blend; set in the fridge **4–5 hrs** on butter paper → cube or grate. **It's not processed cheese**, so it won't fully "set"; keep airtight in the fridge **2–3 days**, make fresh.
9. **Multivitamin tomato ketchup.** 3 tomatoes + 1 small onion (optional) + beetroot + red pumpkin + apple + garlic. Pressure-cook with **just enough water to submerge** (don't over-water — you want a thick puree), **4–5 whistles**; blend (beautiful color from beetroot + red pumpkin); **strain** out extra water (so the mixer doesn't overflow), then add water back to adjust; cook on the stove, stirring, to **ketchup consistency**.
10. **Curd (live demo + Ayurveda notes).** Curd is actually **warm in tendency, not cold** (the "curd is cold" belief is reversed). **Eat curd in all seasons except summer**, and **only between ~11 am–4 pm** (not evening). **Buttermilk** can be taken anytime — take it for **12 months**, it does wonders. **No curd with cough/cold.** Use **fresh homemade curd, not ready-made**; **Greek yogurt** is fine **max twice a week** (it's flavored/dry-fruit-loaded), not daily. *Method:* warm milk + **1–1.5 tsp starter curd** (more in cold season), **whisk vigorously until a foam forms** (crucial step — store-bought yogurt uses added bacteria for texture; the foam does it here), cover, set. *(Curd-making technique shown across* **[00:16:02]**, **[00:21:06]**, **[00:24:04]***.)*
11. **Paneer (live demo).** Boil milk (**packet, cow, or buffalo all work**). Squeeze **fresh lemon in limited quantity on a low flame and stop the moment it curdles** — this keeps the paneer **not sour, so no washing needed** (too much lemon → sour → must rinse off). Strain (**save the whey**). *Flavored paneer:* mix turmeric + chilli flakes + oregano + salt + black pepper + garlic paste + dry fenugreek (with lemon in one hand, masala in the other). *Market-style cubes hack:* put the strained paneer in a bowl, place another **water-filled bowl on top as a weight for ~15 min** → neat square pieces. *Storage:* 1 day → as is; 2 days → keep the paneer **submerged in water** in an airtight glass container in the fridge (wash before use). *(Curdling/technique shown at* **[00:24:04]**, **[00:25:44]**, **[00:26:28]**, **[00:29:02]***.)* Example serving: the instructor's older daughter cuts the pieces after school, sautes in ghee with chilli flakes + oregano, and finishes 8–10 pieces.
