# 2.17 — Working Mothers: Smart Tips for Smooth Solid Food Introduction

## Manage your guilt and emotion first
- There's **no place for guilt** in a working mother's life. Becoming a mother doesn't mean forgetting yourself — *"a mother has to be alive to be a good mother."* A happy, positive working mother who comes home with a smile is better than a sad stay-at-home mother. Don't carry guilt if the child didn't eat or cried for a day.
- **Don't call repeatedly** to check ("did the child eat/sleep?"). **Trust the caregiver** (good for them and you). If new, **train** them; in daycare use cameras, but don't obsess over a "perfect" upbringing.
- You **can** be firm on the non-negotiables (no distraction feeding, no force feeding) regardless of who feeds.

## Practical food management
- **Cook the whole day's food in the morning**, store in **airtight containers**; the caregiver feeds it. (Reheating once in the microwave is fine — radiation concerns apply only to repeated/long reheating, which we're not doing.)
- If someone at home cooks, **plan things in separate cooker vessels** and tell them what to cook and when.
- **Use weekends** to plan the next week, check stock, and pre-store: smoothie/soup ingredients in **zip-lock bags**; keep **flours stored in the fridge** so they're ready at meal time.
- **Introduce the working-hours meal FIRST** when starting solids. If your job is e.g. 11 am–6 pm, set those meals first, and introduce a **snack before dinner** (you can give dinner at home).

## Milk while working
- Bottle/lactogen is **not compulsory**. Check workplace permission to **express and store breastmilk** (express-milk bag / office fridge → use next day; an **extra-knowledge video** covers how to store breastmilk).
- If that's not possible, introduce **lactogen/formula before the job starts** so the child gets used to it.
- Give the **Cerelac-type backup at least once a week** so the caregiver can manage if the child won't eat.

## Build your team
- As in the **Playdate** course (185 activities), have **grandparents/caregivers watch the videos** so they understand *what* is being done, *why*, and the *benefits*, and can play/feed productively.
- **You are your own strongest team** — how you talk to yourself decides how confident a mother you are, and the child senses it.
- **Quality over quantity of time:** even half an hour of true quality time (e.g., the instructor watches her children's practice and they celebrate the day together) counts. Respect for the mother's work matters too.
- Even a homemaker is a "working mother." Cuddle the child, spend quality time, and hold the belief: **"my child will do something amazing in life."** *Parents are co-authors of a child's destiny — we start the sentence, and God completes it through the child.*
