# 2.20 — 10 Tricks to Ensure the Child Eats Independently

**Why independent eating matters:** when a child eats happily, with excitement, by their own hands, the **satisfaction is very different** from being fed. The goal is a **positive association** with self-feeding. It's a great habit (though not strictly mandatory). Works for **6–12 months or toddlers** — start whenever you begin. (Presenter: Shrreya Shah — childbirth educator & pediatric dietitian, under MyDvija; courses cover weaning, BLW, toddler nutrition, overall child development, plus personal consultations.)

1. **Ask the child to feed *you*.** "Mmm, yummy, very tasty!" The child learns to feed and gets motivated to try eating themselves. (What we *do* matters more than what we *say*.)
2. **Parallel play.** While you feed the child, have the child **feed a doll** ("your doll is so hungry!"). Bridges to reality and makes self-feeding a natural transition — key for brain development.
3. **Vertical / popsicle-style plating.** Stack pieces on a fork — steamed carrot, cheese cube, broccoli, cauliflower. Children love the look and are motivated to eat.
4. **Explore different cutlery.** Forks with a bunny/face, colorful or wooden plates, **vacuum plates**, plates with **four pockets** — creative cutlery promotes independent eating.
5. **Change the place.** The child shouldn't feel "I sit here and get fed." Switching the spot promotes independent eating.
6. **No compulsion to sit the child in front.** You can keep the child **in your lap** and let them self-feed.
7. **Eat the food yourself and describe it.** "See what mom put in her mouth — red watermelon, so juicy, mmm!" Encourages the child through the stages (touch → play → throw → smell → mouth → chew).
8. **Preloaded spoon / preloaded hand.** For a child who won't hold the spoon: load food onto the spoon, hand it over, and motivate them to bring it to the mouth.
9. **Scooping activity.** Practice scooping with a spoon during **clay play** or with small objects — the wrist movement of "scoop into the spoon" transfers to eating.
10. **Build excitement around food (like a swimming pool).** Take the child to the **kitchen, fruit/vegetable shopping, plants**; let them **touch and experience** fruits/vegetables and their natural colors.

**Reframe:** instead of *"my child only eats puree,"* recognize *"I have only given my child puree."* When teaching independence, prefer **easy-to-eat foods the child can manage by hand.**
