# 2.16 — How to Help the Baby Eat Independently (BLW Tips)

Common questions for parents doing exclusive BLW (baby-led weaning):

- **When can BLW start?** At **6 months**, once the child ticks all the readiness signs (see lesson 1.3).
- **Does BLW mean only finger food?** No. In the early stage, if the child can't yet manage liquid food or drink from a glass, give **properly cooked finger food that won't slip from the hand** — that's easiest.
- **Can you combine bowl feed with finger food?** **Yes, 100%.** Make the bowl feed a **thick consistency** the child can eat by hand or via a preloaded spoon.
- **Will the child finish an attractively plated meal (rice balls + 2–3 pieces of bread + sides)?** No — and that's fine. Eating bread *dipped in* vegetables, then salad, then more, is a skill for much older children. **How you arrange the plate matters** more than expecting it to be finished.

## When can the child eat the family's food?
- **Ideally after 11 months**, the child eats the **same food made for the family** (just customize if your food is very spicy/oily/fried).
- Even **before 11 months** you can give the same food, adapted. **Example** family meal → child's plate:
  - Bhakri → three small pieces.
  - Paneer-and-peas sabzi → made **without chili**, lightly crushed.
  - Kakdi (cucumber) salad → a few pieces.
  - **Two balls of dal-rice.**
  - The child's plate then mirrors the parent's plate. Set aside the child's portion **before** adding family spices.

## The mindset
Independent eating is a **treat** for the child. **Train** the child through small challenges (not eating for a few days) and handle it with a **smile and patience** — that's the key to an independent eater. Don't feed the child; let them eat independently. Even if it's going well only by ~8 months, that's still a **win-win**.
