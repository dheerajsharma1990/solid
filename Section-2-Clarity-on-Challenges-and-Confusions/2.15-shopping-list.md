# 2.15 — Shopping List: Items to Buy for Solid Food Introduction

*The video shows product photographs from the team (starting ~**[00:00:12]**) so you can see the recommended **features** — no specific brand is promoted; refer to the on-screen photos for what each item looks like.*

Buy these and no more than this:

1. **Preloaded spoon** — must be **BPA-free**, **soft plastic** that won't injure the hand, with a **big, thick, non-slick handle** that fits the child's palmar grip.
2. **Attractive bowl, preferably vacuum bowl** — suction keeps the plate from moving so the child can eat from it in place.
3. **Bib — silicone-based** (easy to clean; cloth bibs let liquid/saliva soak through), ideally with a **small tray** at the bottom to catch food.
4. **Big plastic mat** — under the chair/high chair to catch and clean spills easily.
5. **High chair or booster chair:**
   - **High chair** if the family eats at a dining table (child feels they're eating *with* you).
   - **Booster chair** if the family eats on the floor (brings the child to your eye level).
   - If buying only one new thing, **buy the high chair.**
6. **Pre-loved sources** — local "pre-love" WhatsApp groups, Facebook pages, or pre-love sites (also on their site) sell good gently-used children's products. Optional, your choice.
7. **Fruit nibbler (must-buy)** — put fruits you can't give directly (grapes, pomegranate) inside; the child gets juice/teething benefit while the pulp/garbage stays contained. **Choose a silicone-net nibbler, not a cloth-net one** (much easier to clean).
8. **Spoon-bottle** — a bottle with a spoon at the front and a lid; press the bottle, food comes into the spoon, feed, repeat. Great for travel or leaving the child with grandparents.
9. **Sippers (buy two)** — both should release water **only when the child presses/handles it** (so it doesn't pour out and soak the child). One for plain water, one for the alternate (e.g., neem water).
10. **Open cup** — to teach drinking water (or milk) **without** a sipper; usable from the **6th month**. Like the small thick "cutting-tea" glass in India — transparent, with a textured design for grip, so the parent can see how much the child sips and whether it's too hot. Cheap (~₹15–20) but very useful.
