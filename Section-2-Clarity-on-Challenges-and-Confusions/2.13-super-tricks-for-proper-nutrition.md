# 2.13 — Super (Out-of-the-Box) Tricks for Proper Nutrition

1. **Walnut + almond powder, done right:** either freeze the nuts 2–3 hours before grinding, or lightly roast then grind — the powder turns out better. **Store in the fridge** to keep it tasty and healthy for many days.

2. **Bitter food / Neem water (for liver & internal organs):** A South African pediatrician's research (papers findable on Google) shows eating something **bitter daily** keeps the liver and internal organs healthy. Since few will feed bitter gourd to a child in the first 2 years, add bitterness via:
   - **Boiled bitter gourd** as a finger food (to a child, every taste is new — good exposure).
   - **Neem water:** keep **two sippers** — one plain filtered water, one with **4–5 neem leaves soaked** (no boiling; just soak). Alternate plain and neem water. If no fresh neem, use **neem powder** or **yellow fenugreek (methi) seed powder** — but methi can cause constipation, so use carefully.
   - Benefits: strengthens the liver, **faster recovery when sick**, and makes medicines act more positively on the body. **Note on water:** the child doesn't need extra water — drinking ~8–10 times a day is enough (a full water video is in the extra-knowledge segment).

3. **Avocado** — a combination of **18 nutrients**. Give as a spread or finger food (no cooking). Optional; if unavailable where you live, there's no problem — just a good addition if accessible.

4. **Quinoa & barley** (popular in the West) — soak briefly; using **1–4 tsp** of quinoa in a meal gives good **fiber and protein**.

5. **Milk products before 11 months (no animal milk):** give **almond / soy / coconut milk**. **Curd** is allowed from **6 months**; **cheese** from **8 months**.

6. **Egg introduction:** the **yolk** is a possible allergen and harder to digest. Can technically introduce at 6–7 months, but **8 months is better**. Give a little yolk at breakfast; if the child is fine all day, you can move to boiled egg / omelette. If within the next **8 hours** there are rashes or severe vomiting → it's an allergy → stop and retry after a **1-month break**. (The *Baby's Yum Food Diary* has a chart for recording the body's reaction to each newly introduced food.)

7. **Berries** (strawberry, cherry, blueberry, blackberry, etc.) — important for **immunity, health, and iron absorption**. Include in the diet somehow.
   - **Simple smoothie/porridge recipe** (the course editor's pre-workout breakfast, adapted for babies): **oats + almond milk + dates** (dates replace the honey adults use) **+ any fruit + a little chia seeds**, blended into a **thick puree**. Oats=cereal, fruit=fiber, almond-milk/dates/chia=protein & brain food. Great to feed via a **bottle-spoon** when on the go.

8. **Moringa / drumstick** — add to the child's food (and **every mother's food**). Either put **drumstick in daily dal**, or use **moringa powder** (a pinch or two; available from MyDvija, BigBasket, Amazon). Prevents **B12 deficiency** in mother and child.
