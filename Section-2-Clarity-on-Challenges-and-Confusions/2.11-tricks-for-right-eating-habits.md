# 2.11 — Tricks to Give the Child the Right Eating Habits

The easy "mantra" for building right habits:

1. **No fear, no bribe.** Don't threaten ("the watchman/crow will take your food," "I'll lock you in the bathroom") and don't bribe ("finish food → get chocolate"). A child's life is far bigger than how much food they eat. Fear attached to food will resurface in many areas of life; the child should eat **food for food's sake**, not for chocolate.

2. **Taste buds take time.** A taste develops after eating a food roughly **18–21 times**. (Adults often refuse a vegetable only because a parent always said they didn't like it — habit, not real dislike.)

3. **Change your sentences (don't label).** Instead of *"my child doesn't like sweets,"* (which the child internalizes) say *"my child's taste for sweets hasn't developed yet"* / *"my child is still learning to eat sweets."* This is the right way to instil good habits.

4. **Give food attractive names.** The same dish sounds far more appealing with a creative name — e.g., "Tandoori BBQ garlic-flavor paneer tikka" vs plain paneer; "smoky palak dal with rice," "green porridge," "Red Beauty." Good names make the child want to eat.

5. **Prepare the child for the meal.** Announce it ("In 10 minutes we'll have lunch — do you know what we made today? Let me show you" — *the dish is shown on screen at* **[00:02:53]**). **Involve the child** in serving and making — let them hold/feel the tomato for tomato soup, experience the bright colors. Big benefit for habit-building.

6. **Be clear and give limited, doable options — not infinite choice.** Don't make 5 dishes per meal hoping one is eaten (not practical). "Whatever is made will be eaten." Offer real choices: *eat with mom's or dad's hand? high chair or booster? red plate or blue plate?* But **don't** offer non-options (whether to eat lunch at all, whether to drink mother's milk when it's not allowed).

## The biggest rule: parents must model the habit
The child copies the parent. To inculcate a habit, the parent must have it:
- Don't leave food on the plate; take **small bites, chew well**, don't stuff the whole thing in.
- If you eat looking at the mobile, or drink lots of water while eating, the child will copy that too.
- **Grip development:** until ~9 months the palmar grip is still developing; after ~9 months the **pincer grip** develops and the child picks up and eats food.
