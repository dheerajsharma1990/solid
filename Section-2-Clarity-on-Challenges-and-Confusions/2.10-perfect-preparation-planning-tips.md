# 2.10 — Perfect Preparation: Tips for Planning the Baby's Diet

How to become a better-planned parent so the child can be managed easily even when left with grandparents, daycare, or the father:

1. **Keep flours ready at home** — both combination flours and plain flours, so anything can be made quickly.
2. **Keep attractive plates and bowls** — preferably **vacuum plates/bowls** (they don't move from their place). Have the necessary cutlery on hand.
3. **Keep a plastic sheet** under the feeding spot to contain the mess and make cleaning easy.
4. **Plan meals in advance (in your mind), not on the spot.** Plan after waking up — don't just walk into the kitchen and decide.
5. **Play with natural colors** across the day (e.g., green in the morning, beetroot at noon, kiwi in a snack). More color variety → better immunity and more taste exposure.
6. **No taste-scheduling** — keep variety in **taste and texture**. If the child suddenly stops eating or loses interest, **change the texture and the style of presentation** — that re-creates the mood to eat.
7. **Plan snacks** — keep finger food, sticks, laddu, cheese cubes ready at home for emergencies when the child won't eat anything.
