# 2.14 — Challenges & Solutions (Gagging, Choking, Allergies, etc.)

## Gagging vs. Choking (ground-and-sky difference — know it)
- **Gagging:** the child **makes sounds/different noises**; the tongue reflex is pushing food out. **Rule: do NOT help / do NOT put your finger in** — that can turn gagging into choking. Wait; the child will throw the food out or manage it.
- **Choking:** something is stuck in the **air pipe** (not the food pipe). Signs: **skin turns blackish/bluish, no sound, can't breathe.** Foods that cause it: hard nuts (almond, pistachio, cashew, walnut) eaten directly, a hard bean, or **whole grapes / soft whole items**.

### Choking first aid
1. Hold the child's chin, turn the child **face-down toward gravity**, and give **5 back blows** (between the shoulders) with the heel of the hand.
2. If not out in 5, **turn the child over** and give firm **chest pushes (3 times)**.
3. Repeat **5 back + 3 front** until the object comes out. If still stuck, take the child to **emergency / call emergency services**, but keep doing the 5-back/3-front cycle until help arrives.

## Constipation
Pushing with pain isn't the problem (muscles aren't developed yet); **hard, tight stool is** the problem. (A separate remedies video exists.) Common causes & fixes:
- **Not enough fiber** in food → add fiber.
- **Not enough hydration** → breastmilk/formula/water added to food.
- Add **natural laxatives**: papaya, potato starch, prunes.
- Give the child **lots of tummy time.**

## "Child not interested in food / stays hungry for hours"
The child isn't getting **tired** — kept lying down all day. (Adults eat because they're active.) The companion course **Playdate** teaches parents **185+ activities** for 3-month to 3-year-olds: baby yoga, workouts, stretches; emotional, spiritual, cognitive, holistic development. A tired child eats well.

## Allergens
Possible allergens: **animal milk, eggs, fish** — plus anything a **family member** is allergic to (be extra careful). Introduce gently and **observe the next 8 hours**: severe vomiting, or red grains/rashes = an alarm → **cut that food, don't give it again**.

## Gases / colic
Normal after starting solids. Causes: foods that **grow in soil** (e.g., onion) release gas during digestion; **too much milk / not enough food**. **Rule: milk must never become the child's food** (mother's milk, lactogen, bottle, or expressed milk — nutrition comes from *eating*). Fixes: lots of **tummy time**, properly introduce **fiber**, and reduce the specific gas-causing foods.

## Breast engorgement (when child eats solids)
As the child eats well and asks for breastfeed less, breasts get heavy/tight — normal. **Don't express the milk out** (it keeps supply high). Relief:
- **Chilled cabbage leaves** (kept in freezer) inside the bra.
- A **rice sock**: fill a (husband's) sock with ~½ kg raw rice, tie tightly, microwave **30 sec**, apply.
- Or warm-water application.
- Supply takes **10–15 days** to adjust to lower demand. At night milk may leak — put a **plastic sheet under the bedsheet**.

## Sleep disturbance
Myth: "child will sleep through the night once they eat solids" / "extra food at 10 am → sleeps all night." **False.** Small babies wake for many reasons — feed, diaper, pee/potty, toilet cleaning, dreaming, **thirst**, memory, or being smart enough to know night-feeding gets attention (especially with working mothers). Fix: proper **sleep training** (no need to cry-it-out; many gentle methods; a separate sleep-training course exists). Give **water slowly at night** and gradually reduce night-feed duration.

## Bottle / lactogen dependence
A child who eats then immediately wants the milk bottle = a strict no-no. Plan:
- At **9–10 months**, lovingly **donate away all milk bottles**; a week before, start giving milk in an **open cup** (morning/afternoon).
- Re-introduce milk **without** the bottle; by **1 year stop lactogen**; **after 11 months no bottle at all.**

## "Fighting 20 days, child still not eating anything"
Try **Mama-Milk Rice**: cook rice very well, add **expressed mother's milk / lactogen / formula** — a *known taste* for the child. Use only for a few days (risk: child then seeks that taste in everything), but okay if nothing else is working.

## "Used to eat well, suddenly stopped"
Find the reason:
- **Major life change** (weather change, house/location shift, moving to a nuclear family).
- **Recovering from illness** (cold, cough, fever).
- **Teething** (lots of irritation → can't eat). The course links **YouTube extra-knowledge videos**, including a **teething series** to watch. Fix: **change texture and presentation** of food.

## Potty changes
Many normal changes after solids: different colors (red from beetroot, green from spinach, brown, yellow), thread-like potty, **food pieces visible**, foam — **all normal** (seeing the food you fed in the potty is a positive sign). **Abnormal / worry signs:** **dark black tar-like** potty, **visible blood** (not beetroot), or **watery loose motions >14–15 times**. New adult-like smells are also normal.

## Vomiting
Often from **overfeeding** ("ate half a bowl, gave more, brought it back up"). **No guilt** — we've not raised 15–20 kids; the child is learning and so are we. Vomiting shows the child's **self-protective mechanism is working**. If the child wants more, feed; if not, soothe with breastmilk/lactogen.

## Weight (recap)
Weight stuck / suddenly thinner is normal — the rapid first-5-month gain never repeats, and height increases making the child look thin. **Target: ~triple birth weight near the first birthday.** Don't over-stress.

## Sick child not eating
Very common. The *Baby's Yum Food Diary* has a **"sick food"** segment. Offer: **turmeric milk, tulsi kaada, hot soups, some crispy food.** A sick child won't eat normal amounts and usually prefers breast/bottle (pacifying effect) — that's fine. Even ~500 g loss in a week is normal; regaining may take 1–2 months.

## Closing reassurance
These challenges appear in **every** parent's journey, up to ~**1–1.25 years**, then reduce. Stay peaceful and relaxed; accept with grace that you're doing your best for the child.
