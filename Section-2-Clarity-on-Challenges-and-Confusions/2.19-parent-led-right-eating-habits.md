# 2.19 — Developing Right Eating Habits: What I Must Do as a Parent

Ask yourself: **"Am I leading as a parent?"**

## 1. The child copies you (model the habit)
Whatever habit you want — eat well, chew well, return the plate to its place — **you must do it yourself.** (Example: a parent who picks vegetables out of pulao can never credibly tell the child to eat vegetables; so the instructor took on that role herself.) Draw on parental authority where you can — it makes teaching easy.

## 2. Attention & appreciation (what you focus on, the child repeats)
- Stop saying *"my child doesn't eat anything."* (One mother said it 10 times in 15 minutes — yet the child ate plain chapati, plain fruit, plain porridge.) The accurate sentence is *"my child doesn't eat **the way I want** (e.g., veggies on bread)."* A child not eating "veggies-on-bread" is **not** malnourished.
- **Be creative instead.** Hide the 13 vegetables + cereal-protein-fiber in foods the child *does* like — cheese/paneer **paratha**, **momos**, **cutlets** (one mother fit 13 veggies into momos/cutlets). Adapt to the child's preferred style; it's an art.
- **Praise specifically:** "How well you eat bread and fruit! Combine them and bread becomes a vegetable." "How well you take soup / steamed vegetables!" Focusing on "he doesn't eat" plants exactly that idea in the child.

## 3. Involve the child & show the effort (no guilt-tripping)
- Involve the child in **making and shopping** for food — take them to the vegetable vendor so they understand the process; tasty food isn't made by chance.
- **Don't** complain "I worked so hard and you don't eat" (no drama, no guilt). **Do** narrate it excitingly: "Do you know what mom made? I took vegetables, added soya butter, steamed it…", "I shaped your paratha into **7 small hearts**," "today it's not plain idli, it's **baby masala idli**." This makes mealtime exciting for both.

## 4. Protect interpersonal relationships (marriage first)
- The instructor does ~10 counselling sessions a week and sees many couples. **Don't let the spouse feel replaced by the child.** Remember **who came into your life first — the husband/wife.** Give that role importance: sometimes leave the child and go out as a couple, sometimes pack the child's food and go to the mall together.
- **Travel with children is easy** with prep: carry a **travel cooker / smart kitchen**, pre-made **flours**, a case for making **idli/banana** items, an **airtight container**, and **instant homemade Cerelac** (just add hot water). (A full **travel-food** segment is in the *Yum Food Diary*.)
- Children learn **respect, love, and how to treat family by watching the parents** — it's never "you must respect me." A child should see *"my parents love each other."* Invest in this **as a couple/family**.

## 5. Don't take tension; keep food in perspective
- Grandparents giving chocolates/chivda/sev do not do permanent damage — **only parents can.** Food is **just a part** of the child's life, not the whole of it.
- Invest in **quality time, developmental play, outings**, and your **marriage**.
