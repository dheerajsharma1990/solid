# Q&A — Starting Solids at 6 Months 15 Days

Personal Q&A extracted from the MyDvija Solid-Food-Introduction course summaries (`summaries/solid/`), 2026-06-06.
Baby context: 6 months 15 days old, breastfed, follows feed → activity → sleep routine, fed after waking. All readiness signs reported ticked except independent sitting (mother plans to hold baby in lap).

---

## Q1. Baby is 6 months 15 days — what can I begin with, and what should the next 4 weeks look like?

### ⚠️ First: the sitting readiness sign (Lesson 1.3)

The course requires **all** readiness signs ticked. Sign #3 is sitting ability, but the bar is lower than "sitting with support":

> The baby should be able to **sit, even for a few seconds, or hold a slight bend, without the neck/body collapsing**. If the baby flops, drops the neck and cannot sit at all, **do not start solids — not ready yet.**

- Solid neck control + can hold a slightly-bent upright posture in the lap for a few seconds without flopping → **passes**; upright lap-feeding is fine.
- Truly flops / neck drops → course says **wait**, age notwithstanding; confirm with the pediatrician. At 6.5 months you are not "late" — the rule is start *after* the 6th month.
- Lap-feeding caution: the classic "child in lap, mouth held open, spoon pushed in" is **force feeding — the #1 wrong method** (Lesson 1.2). Hold him upright, but let him see, touch, and lead.

### Week 1 — Lunch only (the only meal introduced first)

**Days 1–3: stomach preparation ("water before food")**

| Day | Give |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Rice + dal water** — 1 tsp rice + 1 tsp dal + turmeric (no salt!), 3 whistles; 2–4 spoons of the top liquid. Unpolished rice that overcooks soft. |
| 2 | **Whole moong water** — cooked with turmeric, ~2 spoons of the water |
| 3 | **Dal-rice / moong, hand-smashed** — slightly thick |

Milk continues normally before and after — these spoons only *signal the stomach*, they don't feed.

**Days 4–7: proper lunch**

- **Every meal = cereal + protein + fiber.** Lunch: dal (protein) + rice (cereal) + vegetable (fiber).
- **The 5 allowed dals:** yellow moong, green moong, black urad, white urad, masoor. (Toor ~8 months, chana ~11 months.)
- Cook dal and rice **separately**; blend the dal; hand-smash — **never grind/puree** over 6 months (gums/tongue/cheeks chew; teeth not needed).
- **Timing:** when "happily hungry" — ~30 min before the next milk demand, in the 12–2 pm window, same window daily.
- **Quantity:** 2–4 spoons fine, a bowl fine — parent's job is to **offer**; how much he eats is his decision.
- **Day 5 checkpoint:** still throwing/spitting everything = lunch "not set" — continue patiently, don't add another meal.

### Week 2 — Set lunch, add dinner

- **"Set" = 3–4 bites with visible excitement** (~by day 12, ~5 happy bites = set).
- **Dinner** (7–8:30 pm, or ~6:30 if he won't eat late): same rice-based non-allergenic combos. Alternate khichdi lunch / dal-rice dinner, then swap.
- **Avoid repetition — three levers:** change the dal (6 options incl. mixed), the vegetable (drumstick pulp = star, great B12; also carrot, peas, pumpkin, bottle gourd, spinach, beetroot), the ghee tadka (cumin, curry leaves, garlic+spinach, ajwain+red pumpkin, coriander). ~25–30 lunch varieties from dal-rice alone.
- **No taste schedule** — he should never be able to predict the food.

### Week 3 — Add breakfast (8–9:30 am)

- Wake → milk → breakfast when next happily hungry. (Wakes late → skip straight to breakfast; don't let milk push breakfast to 11.)
- **Prep flours in the fridge:** roasted-and-powdered oats, rice, bajra, dalia, ragi *sattva* (sprouted: soak overnight → sprout under wet cloth → shade-dry → roast → grind; big calcium/iron boost), khichdi-mix flour.
- Two templates from any flour:
  - **Sweet kheer:** ghee → roast flour → **dry-dates powder** (protein + iron + sweetness — *not* sugar/honey) → grated apple/pear/mashed banana. + ½ tsp roasted walnut-almond powder = brain food.
  - **Upma:** ghee → cumin + curry leaves → rava/oats → roasted Bengal-gram (sattu) powder (protein) → mashed veg (fiber).

### Week 4 — Consolidate all three meals

- Goal by ~7.5 months: **breakfast, lunch, dinner all set** at fixed windows (±30 min flex) — matches the course's "all three meals usually set by ~7 months."
- **No snacks yet** — snacks (11 am / 4 pm / 6 pm, any two) come only after all three meals are set (~8 months).
- **Weight:** ~110 g/week at 6–9 months; a 1–2 month stagnation phase is normal (energy goes to new skills). Stagnant = allowed, loss = not.

### Strict no's for the whole period

- ❌ No salt, no sugar, no honey, no cow/buffalo/goat milk until 11–12 months
- ❌ No puree/mash/grind, no force feeding, no distraction/screen/rhyme feeding
- ✅ Milk (breast/formula) **stays primary nutrition** all year — food is secondary
- ✅ Combine **preloaded spoon + letting him touch food** (bowl feeding + BLW); mess and gagging are the expected part of learning
- ✅ Colorful plate, feed seated and settled, never become "the spoon person" he cries at

## Q2a. For days 1–7, is it still a 4-spoon serving?

- **Days 1–3 (prep waters):** yes, just **2–4 spoons** of the liquid — deliberately tiny: *"the 2 spoons won't fill the stomach — they just signal the stomach that something else is coming."*
- **Days 4–7 (proper lunch):** **no fixed quantity** — 2–4 spoons is a reassurance floor, not a cap:

> "No research dictates quantity at this age. A full bowl is great; 2–4 spoons is also fine — *the child is learning to eat*. It is the parent's job to **offer** the right food at the right time; whether and how much the child eats is the child's decision — respect it."

Progress is measured in *enthusiasm*, not volume — a meal is "set" at **3–4 bites with visible excitement**.

## Q2b. How should I plan breastfeeds around lunch (feed–activity–sleep routine)?

From Lessons 3.27 + 1.4:

- **Offer solids ~1.5–2 hours after a breastfeed** — lunch lands ~30 min *before* the next milk demand ("happily hungry": not just-fed, not over-hungry).
- **Yes, breastfeed after lunch if he wants it:** *"Milk continues like normal before and after lunch. If the child wants breastfeed right after these few spoons, give it — no problem."* Milk stays primary nutrition all year.
- Routine becomes: **wake → breastfeed → activity → lunch (~1.5–2 h after that feed) → breastfeed if he asks → sleep.** Lunch slots into the activity block.
- **Caution as weeks pass:** don't give a full feed *every time he spits food* — he learns "milk is coming." If he eats 2–5 bites and it isn't feed time, **re-offer the same meal after ~½ hour** (fresh food only).
- Early-solids feed count: **~3–5 day feeds + 2–3 night feeds.**
- Lunch window is **12–2 pm** — 2 pm is the edge; never let it drift to 3.

## Q2c. Guidelines for giving water? (Bonus Lesson 5.41)

- Water starts **with solids**: **1 sip every 1–2 hours after eating**; **~8–10 sips/day total** (breastmilk is ~80% water; he already gets ~1 L fluid from milk + dal + food).
- **Plain filtered water, room temperature** — not fridge-cold, not warmed. Don't re-boil-and-cool filtered water; boil-and-cool only if no filter.
- **Introduce by spoon → move to a sippy cup** (soft spout, no-leak valve, grip handles; self-drinking ~7–8 months). Keep it lidded.
- **Not much water mid-meal** (fills the small stomach) — sips between/after meals.
- **Never sugar or honey water.** If he resists plain water later: occasional 1:10 diluted fresh juice or coconut water — but he must still learn plain water's taste.
- Optional course tip: a second sipper with 3–4 fresh neem leaves soaked (not boiled), alternated occasionally — bitter exposure claimed to support liver/immunity.

## Q3. Can whole moong (mashed) be given from day 4?

**Yes — the course has it from day 3:** *"dal-rice / moong, hand-smashed — give slightly thick water/mash."*

- **Whole (green) moong is one of the 5 allowed dals** from the start — the gentlest; it's the day-2 stomach-prep water.
- **The peel is a feature:** Bonus 5.39 (constipation) recommends moong/green-moong/masoor **with peel** for fiber.
- **All moong varieties are good first-year foods** (Lesson 3.24: green/yellow/black moong allowed within the first year; good weight gainers).

How to do it right:

1. **Cook very soft** — cooker, 3 whistles, with turmeric. **No salt** until ~11 months.
2. **Hand-smash, don't blend/grind** — no-puree rule over 6 months. Fork / pav-bhaji-masher texture, not paste.
3. **Consistency thick** — like idli batter; shouldn't run off the spoon.
4. **Balance:** moong alone isn't a complete meal — pair with rice (cereal) + a soft veg like bottle gourd or carrot (fiber) = the course's dal-rice pattern for days 4–7.
