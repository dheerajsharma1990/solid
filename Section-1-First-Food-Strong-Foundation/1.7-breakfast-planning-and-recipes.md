# 1.7 — Breakfast: How to Plan & What to Offer (20–30 varieties)

Breakfast assumes a **rushed morning**, so keep **flours ready in the fridge** in advance. (A separate course **PDF** lists which flours to prep.)

## Flours to keep ready
Plain cereal flours: **jaggery-grain flour, bajra (pearl millet), rice, oats** (always **roast/process** grains then powder them), **dalia (broken wheat)**, **ragi sattva (nachni satva)** and **wheat sattva**.
- **Don't buy sattva from the market** — make at home (or buy MyDvija's, made the same way, no preservatives, not instant/ready-to-eat).
- **How to make sattva (sprouted flour):** soak the grain (ragi/wheat) **overnight** → drain → keep in a pot covered with a **wet cloth all day**, shaking/turning periodically → small **sprouts** appear by day's end → **dry in shade** on a thin cloth → **roast** → grind to powder.
  - Wheat is a possible allergen (gluten); this sprouting process makes it easier to digest for the child.
  - Sprouting ragi greatly improves **calcium and iron** availability. So ragi and wheat should be used as **sattva**.
- **Combination flours to keep:** "khichdi mix" (dal + moong dal + rice, rice soaked overnight, drained, roasted, powdered); **multigrain** (all the above grains + the 5 dals, soaked, roasted, powdered).
- **Logic:** with a **plain cereal flour** you must add protein + fiber; with a **mixed flour** you only need to add fiber.

## Two things to make from any flour: **sweet kheer** or **upma**

### Method (kheer)
Pan/wok → ghee (tempering) → add the flour → roast it. Then choose kheer or upma.
- **Sweet kheer example (oats):** roast oats flour → add **protein**: **dry dates powder / dates paste** (boosts iron + protein and adds sweetness) → add **fiber**: grated apple, mashed banana, or grated pear (cooks because grated; no blender needed). = cereal + protein + fiber.
- **On sweeteners:** sugar only after 9–11 months; jaggery is "equally bad" (sugar is more processed, so worse) and doesn't raise hemoglobin much — prefer **dry dates** for sweetness + iron.
- **Brain food add-in:** roast **walnut + almond**, powder; add **½ tsp** to anything sweet → makes it a complete meal (omega-3 fatty acids).
- **Variations from the same flour:** make it thicker (don't cook the flour) → **pancake**; or soak flour in **curd + a pinch of Eno** → small **idlis** (e.g., grated-carrot idlis).

### Method (upma)
Pan → ghee → **cumin powder + curry leaves** → add cereal (e.g., **rava/semolina**, a plain cereal) → roast.
- **Protein for upma:** **roasted Bengal-gram dal powder** — known as *futana chi dal* (Marathi), the dal added to churmura chivda (Hindi), or "roasted Bengal gram dal" (used for green chutney); also called **sattu** flour. Roast lightly, powder, add.
- **Fiber:** any boiled-and-hand-mashed vegetable puree.
- **Key flavor of upma = sweet + sour:** add a little **jaggery** + **2 drops lemon juice**.
- Works with oats, rava, jowar, bajra flour.

## Time-saving / fortification tips
- **No time to grate vegetables?** Skip fiber at breakfast and **make it up with more fiber in snacks.**
- **Stocks (non-allergenic, can give from the start):** boil chicken **without salt** → use the **chicken stock** as the water in upma; or use **vegetable stock**.
- **Make breakfast healthier / weight-gainer:** add **sweet apple/pear/banana puree** (sweet dishes) or **sweet potato / potato puree** (weight gain) — but never compromise the cereal-protein-fiber base.
- **Brain food for savory dishes:** add **2 pinches of flaxseed powder** while cooking.
