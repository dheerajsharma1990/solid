# 1.2 — Right & Wrong Ways to Offer Food / What is Weaning, BLW, Complementary Feeding

## What is weaning?
Weaning = when the child moves from only mother's/formula milk toward eating normal (adult-type) food. Also called **solid food introduction** or **complementary feeding**. Key points:
- You do **not stop** mother's milk or formula. You add the **extra nutrients** the child needs on top of continued milk.
- After solids begin, food does **not** become the child's main nutrition. **Primary nutrition stays milk** (mother's/formula); the food you give is **secondary nutrition**.

## The four ways to feed — two wrong, two right

### ❌ 1. Force feeding (negative)
The cultural method our parents used: child held in the lap, mouth held open, spoons pushed in (often when the child cries). Usually pureed food, pushed in quickly. It is **psychologically harsh** and makes the child's **vision of and liking for food negative**. Not allowed.

### ❌ 2. Distraction feeding (negative)
Feeding while distracting — "look, a crow!", "look at the mobile" (**[00:01:59]**, given as an example of what *not* to do), "A for apple," then slipping a bite in. Three problems:
1. A child engaged in something else never gets the full *experience/effect* of eating.
2. Digestion is never good when eating while distracted, so even good food won't be absorbed well.
3. The child's *concept of food* becomes distorted — e.g. rhymes show "an apple falls from the top," but apples don't fall from the sky. Children must understand food **as it really is**, and that practical understanding only comes from being involved in eating. (Even rhymes-instead-of-movies is still distraction — not allowed.)

### ✅ 3. Bowl feeding (positive)
Food (pureed food or juice) served **in a bowl with a spoon**. Two sub-methods:
- You feed the child from the bowl with a spoon.
- **Preloaded spoon**: you fill a spoon and hand it to the child; the child self-feeds while you load the next spoon.

**The swimming-pool analogy:** you cannot learn to swim by standing outside the pool watching for days — you must get into the water. Likewise, if you always hold the food and never let the child touch the food or spoon, the child can never learn to eat independently. To raise an independent eater, the child **must get the bowl-feeding experience and touch food**.
- It's normal for a beginner to "get water up the nose" — i.e., the child will gag, spill, get messy, get food everywhere (but not always in the mouth) when learning something new. **This is completely okay and expected.**

### ✅ 4. BLW — Baby Led Weaning (positive)
"Baby led" = we don't feed the child; **the child eats with their own hands.** The preloaded-spoon method (above) is itself a form of baby-led eating.

## Recommended approach (combine the two positive methods)
- Use **bowl feeding + BLW together** — it's most effective.
- Give the BLW/independent-eating items **first**, then top up with bowl feeding (e.g., child ate 2–3 bites independently → offer a little more by bowl feed).
- This course works fully even for parents doing **BLW only**.
- The real target is **not** getting food in "by hook or by crook." The target is that the child **eats well, eats with love, eats while engaged**, and gets all the benefits.
