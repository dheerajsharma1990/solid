# 1.4 — What to Offer First / Meal Timing & Planning

## The "happily hungry" rule
Regardless of age, offer food when the child is **happily hungry** — not over-hungry, not full.
- A very hungry child won't eat properly (no mood to experiment); a child who just had milk won't eat either.
- Timing trick: if the child takes milk at 11 am and asks again around 1–1:30 pm, plan **lunch ~half an hour before** the next milk demand (i.e., ~1 pm).

## Fixed meal discipline (parents must be disciplined)
Random timings (breakfast 10, then 12, then 3, then 5…) don't work. Anchor times like adults:
- **Breakfast:** ~8–10 am
- **Lunch:** ~11:30 am–2 pm
- **Dinner:** evening
- Timing can shift **family-wise** by the child's wake time, but stay disciplined.
- Example: child wakes 7 am → give milk first; offer breakfast only when next *happily hungry*. If child wakes 8:30 am, do **not** give milk first (else breakfast slips to 11 am) — that breaks discipline.

## A full day's rhythm
Wake → milk → (when happily hungry) **breakfast** → ~11–11:30 **snack** (skip if sleeping) → **lunch** (~12:30) → nap/play → **4 pm snack** → nap → **6 pm snack** → evening activity/walk → **dinner**. Don't rush all of this at once.

## Order of introducing meals (one meal at a time)
1. **Lunch first.** Reasons: the child usually had milk before lunch, and after lunch there's half a day to observe digestion/problems.
2. After lunch is set → **dinner or breakfast**.
3. After those two are set → the **third meal**.
4. After all three meals → add **snacks** (11 am, 4 pm, 6 pm).

## How long each meal takes to "set"
- No fixed rule. If by day 5 the child still throws/spits and won't touch food, that meal is **not set** — don't add the next meal yet.
- If by ~day 12 the child eats ~5 bites happily, treat lunch as set and move on. **Five bites of food fills more than a bottle of milk** — don't compare them.
- **Broadly, ~1 week per new meal.** So all three meals are usually set by ~7 months; adding 1–2 snacks brings the child to ~8 months.
- **Ideal 8-month-old day:** breakfast, lunch, dinner + any **two** snacks between 11/4/6.

## Meal vs. snack
- **Meal** = nutritional combination of **cereal + protein + fiber** (a course PDF details which foods give what).
- **Snack** = not necessarily cooked; can be simple/independent-eating items. Don't be cooking separately at 11, 4 and 6 — properly cook breakfast/lunch/dinner; keep snacks easy.
