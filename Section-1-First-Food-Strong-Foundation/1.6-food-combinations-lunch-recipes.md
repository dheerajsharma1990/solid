# 1.6 — Easy Food Combinations / Lunch Recipes & the Cooking Method

## The master combination (applies for life)
Every **meal** (breakfast, lunch, dinner) = **Cereal + Protein + Fiber**.
- **Cereal:** wheat, ragi/nachni, rice, oats, etc. ("churmura" type grains).
- **Protein:** all lentils (dal), all whole grains, all dry fruits, **milk & milk products**.
- **Fiber:** all fruits and vegetables.
- (These are broad groupings for easy planning — vegetables also give carbs, cereals give some protein, but keep it simple.)
- A course **PDF** lists which food gives which nutrient.

## Special case: child under 6 months who must start early
Only start early for genuine reasons (working mother, milk already on formula, parent absent, doctor says medically required). **Do not start at 4–4.5 months — wait until at least 5 months.** For these younger babies, **pureed food is allowed** (the only context where puree is okay), because they aren't ready for independent eating.
- Give: purees/pastes/smashed vegetables, soups, smoothies — carrot, bottle gourd, apple, pear, red pumpkin, potato, sweet potato, etc.
- **How to make a puree:** a **1-litre cooker** is a great investment (or use a pot). Cut small pieces (cook fruit *with the peel*), add some water, lid on, **3 whistles**. Mash with a spoon or a quick hand-blender. Nutritionally puree gives little, but it's acceptable when starting early.
- **Mixed veg** (potato, carrot, green peas, French beans) can be cooked together as soup or **thick puree**.
- **Consistency = thick**, like idli batter or thick rice kheer — flowy but not liquid. It shouldn't fall straight off the spoon, and the child should be able to pick it up by hand. (Pomegranate juice = too liquid; offerable but won't satisfy.)

## Child over 6 months — the proper start

### First 3 days: "water" before proper food (stomach preparation)
- **Day 1 — rice/dal water:** in a cooker vessel, 1 tsp rice + 1 tsp dal + turmeric (no salt; salt not allowed until 11 months), **3 whistles**; give 2–4 spoons of the liquid on top. Use rice that **overcooks soft** after 3–6 whistles — **not polished rice**.
- **Day 2 — whole moong water:** cook whole moong with turmeric, give ~2 spoons of water on top. The 2 spoons won't fill the stomach — they just signal the stomach that "something else is coming" (preparation).
- **Day 3 — dal-rice / moong, hand-smashed:** give slightly thick water/mash.
- Milk continues like normal **before and after** lunch. If the child wants breastfeed right after these few spoons, give it — no problem.

### The 5 allowed dals
**Yellow moong, green moong, black urad, white urad, masoor.** Keep this list handy.
- **Toor dal:** after ~8 months. **Chana dal:** after ~11 months. Test at home first; if no colic, it's digestible.

### How much should the child eat?
No research dictates quantity at this age. A full bowl is great; 2–4 spoons is also fine — *the child is learning to eat.*
**Parenting rule:** it is the parent's job to **offer** the right food at the right time with the right nutrients; **whether and how much the child eats is the child's independent decision — respect it.**

## Proper lunch = Bowl food + Finger food (both cooked)
Cooking method using a cooker (separate vessels):
- **Dal** in the lower vessel (1–2 spoons; one dal or a 4–5 dal mix), **rice** in the upper vessel — **cooked separately**, not together.
- **Finger food** in another vessel: steamed broccoli, cauliflower, carrot, baby corn, French beans. Cut carrot into finger-length sticks, dip in water, steam.
- **Right steaming test:** a fork should slide in and out easily; if pressing the fork turns it to paste, it's **overcooked**. If pieces crumble at a touch, use a **steamer** instead of the cooker (DIY: pot + water + a net on top + pieces on the net).
- **Sautéing option:** sauté florets in a little ghee — tasty; sprinkle oregano, pepper, or cumin powder.
- **Anti-slip trick** (so pieces don't slip from the hand): roll steamed carrot in semolina/rava, or in roasted crumbs of cornflakes/wheat flakes/oats.

## ~25–30 lunch varieties from dal-rice (no repetition for weeks)
Vary three levers — **change the dal, change the vegetable, change the tempering (tadka):**

- **Dal + rice:** 5 single dals + 1 mixed-dal = 6 dal options; add a fiber vegetable. Best vegetable = **drumstick** (great B12 — scoop the cooked inner pulp, discard skin). Other adds: carrot, green peas, French beans, red pumpkin, bottle gourd, potato, sweet potato, spinach, tomato, fenugreek. Blend the dal.
- **Temperings (tadka in ghee):** cumin powder; asafoetida *(allowed but in small amount — Ayurveda notes a slightly "tamasic"/anger-inducing effect in excess)*; curry leaves; garlic (pairs with moong + spinach); **carom seeds (ajwain)** for red-pumpkin dal (adds aroma, masks garlic smell); mint leaves; coriander leaves (with bottle gourd + tomato). Gujarati-style: dal with tomato cooked in, tempered, finished with **jaggery** — every day a different color and taste.
- **Khichdi varieties:** mix the 5 dals + rice; add grated **beetroot** (red), **spinach** puree (green), grated **carrot**, grated **bottle gourd (loki)**, or grated **paneer** (paneer khichdi). Dal=protein, rice=cereal, vegetable=fiber → full combination.
- **Other rice combos:** kadhi-khichdi / kadhi-chawal (jowar-flour kadhi with buttermilk, tempered with curry leaves); **curd rice** (fresh home curd + overcooked rice, hand-smashed, consistency adjusted).
- **Easy-pickup tip:** make a thicker dal and form big dal-rice **balls** for the child to lift.

## Plating ("family pot")
Make the plate beautiful: 2–3 dal-rice balls + a steamed/sautéed broccoli + a cauliflower & carrot stick. Combine **finger food + independent eating**, then optionally a bowl of dal-rice (hand-fed, preloaded spoon, or 2–3 bites by you — all fine).

## Dinner
Same rice-based combinations as lunch (everything listed is **non-allergenic** — no eggs/milk used yet, on purpose, so no food allergy). Alternate: khichdi at lunch → dal-rice at dinner, and vice versa; keep changing.
- The day **lunch and dinner are both set**, introduce **breakfast** (next lesson).
