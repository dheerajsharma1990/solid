# 1.9 — Important Things to Remember During Solid Food Introduction (Revision)

A consolidated checklist of non-negotiables:

- **Every meal = cereal + protein + fiber.**
- The child must **eat seated in one place** — don't run after the child to feed.
- **No grinded/pureed food** — teeth aren't needed to chew.
- The child needs **no milk biscuits, no animal milk, no packaged food.**
- **Animal milk** (cow, buffalo, goat) — only **after 11 months.**
  - If you want to give a "milk" earlier, use **soy milk, almond milk, or coconut milk.**
  - **Never** take milk from a carton, add it to kheer and boil it — strict no.
  - **Never** put **powdered (formula) milk or breast milk on the gas/heat** while cooking.

- **Non-veg, introduce carefully:**
  - **Chicken stock** — non-allergenic.
  - **Chicken** — non-allergenic **if very well cooked.**
  - **Fish** — possible allergen.
  - **Mutton** — heavy on digestion.
  - **Egg** — possible allergen.
  - Don't introduce non-veg suddenly; a **separate segment** ("Introducing non-veg to baby") gives full clarity.

- **Salt, sugar and honey** — **none before 11 months.** (The "extra knowledge"/bonus segment gives the scientific reasons.)

Hold these basics together with the earlier lessons before moving on.
