# 1.3 — How to Know the Baby is Ready for Solids

Check **all** of the following before starting solids. Only when every point ticks should you conclude the baby is ready.

## 1. Age
- Per **WHO** (World Health Organization) and **ICMR** (Indian Council of Medical Research), a baby can digest solid food at **6 months**.
- Starting earlier causes problems that show up later as gas, acidity, indigestion.
- **If exclusively breastfed:** start solids **after the 6th month**.
- **If already weaned / on formula** (i.e., already taking something other than mother's milk): you may start when the baby **completes the 5th month (i.e., the 6th month begins)**.
- **Premature babies:** first ask the baby's doctor whether solids can start. If yes, use **corrected age**. Example: a baby that is 6 months old but was born 3 weeks early is effectively 3 weeks short of 6 months — wait until corrected age reaches 6 months.
- Feeding only to "fill the stomach" is a strict no — feed for **nutrient absorption**.

## 2. Tongue/face movement
The 6-month-old moves the face/mouth toward you, the plate, the food — running a "food program" even with nothing in the mouth. A sign of readiness.

## 3. Sitting ability
The baby should be able to **sit, even for a few seconds**, or hold a slight bend, without the neck/body collapsing. If the baby flops, drops the neck and cannot sit at all, **do not** start solids — not ready yet.

## 4. Following food
The baby watches you eat, looks from your plate to their own mouth and back — "following food" — showing genuine interest. A very good sign.

## 5. Mouthing / eating things
The baby puts toys, spoons, plates, fingers in the mouth. This is **important and good** because:
- **Different textures** teach the baby that food has different textures.
- **Different tastes** (plastic, toys, etc.) prepare the baby to accept different-tasting foods.
- It trains the **gag reflex**: a reflex on the tongue that pushes things out so too much doesn't go in. Through mouthing the baby learns **how much** to put in the mouth and how big a piece is comfortable.
- A baby who mouths has the *capacity* to eat what we teach. So mouthing is a very good readiness sign.

## Working-mother exception
If a mother must rejoin work (e.g., 10 am–6 pm) and wants solids established first: **talk to the doctor**. If the doctor agrees, start solids about **4 weeks before the job begins**, especially aligned with working hours, so that when the mother is away someone else can manage the child's food peacefully.
