# 1.5 — What NOT to Do During Solid Food Introduction (Strict No's)

## Part 1 — Food rules

1. **No "taste schedule."** Don't fix sweet every morning, khichdi every evening, pomegranate juice daily at 11, etc. The child should not be able to predict the food; unpredictability is the perfect approach.

2. **No grinded / mashed / pureed / paste food.** Teeth are **not** required to chew — the **tongue, cheeks and gums** do the chewing, and every child has these, so the child *can* chew.

3. **Why pureed/paste is genuinely harmful (not just disallowed):** Chewing and mixing **saliva** with food is essential — *"if the mouth (the first stage) is fine, the stomach will be fine."* Chewing affects the child's **brain, immunity, gas issues and overall body**. Skipping chewing (just opening the mouth and dropping food in) deletes that whole process — not allowed. Chewing well is also important for **clear speech/vocabulary** later. So for proper **growth *and* development**, the child must chew — never feed pureed/paste.

4. **No nutritionally unbalanced food.** E.g., only ragi (nachni) puree, or a thin rice-flour kheer/paste alone, is not acceptable as a meal.

## Part 2 — Plate, presentation & feeding behaviour

- **Choose the right plate and bowl** (covered in detail in the shopping-list lesson). A child *"eats with the eyes first"* — food and tableware must look appealing.
- Use **colorful, bright-colored** plates and bowls to create excitement. If the family insists on a silver bowl, place the **silver bowl inside a colorful one** — child sees color, family sees silver, everyone's happy.
- **Don't be a feared figure.** A mother who only ever appears holding the spoon/bowl (so the child cries on sight) builds a **negative association**. Don't approach already crying.
- **Feed the child seated, not while wandering the house chasing them.** "The food is on this plate, and so am I" — i.e., be present and settled at the meal.

## A note on intuition
Some intuitive things are fine (the instructor still hand-feeds her 8- and 12-year-olds sometimes, and enjoys it). What must be avoided are the practices that are **scientifically wrong** in their effect.
