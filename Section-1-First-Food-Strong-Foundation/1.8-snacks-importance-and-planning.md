# 1.8 — Are Snacks Important & How to Plan Them

## Snack timing & discipline
- Snack slots: **11 am, 4 pm, 6 pm.** ±15 minutes is fine; skipping, delaying, or shifting by hours is not.
- **Parents must be disciplined** — but you may need to **wake the child** to keep meals on schedule.
- **How many snacks:** initially meals come first; ~**8–9 months** the child takes ~1 snack, ~**1 year+** up to 2–3. In the early stage the child is often asleep at one of 11/4/6 — **skip that slot, never force a snack.** Meals have fixed timing; snacks are flexible.

## 11 am snack — pulpy fruits (uncooked)
- **Pulpy fruits:** avocado, banana, chiku (sapota), kiwi, papaya, watermelon, muskmelon.
- Serve as **finger-sized / square pieces** of properly **ripe** fruit. Watermelon: a triangle on the rind (seeds removed). Banana: peel halfway and hold it (full peel makes it slip).
- **Seeing fruit pieces in the potty is normal** — we can't measure how much went in; if it shows in the potty, at least *something* did. The child isn't expected to chew it small first.
- **If no pulpy fruit at home:** Amaranth (Rajgira) **laddu/chikki**; or saute fresh **homemade paneer** pieces in ghee with a little pepper/oregano; or **steamed/boiled sweet potato or potato** (steam partially, then saute in a little ghee → "fries"-like, slightly crispy, easy to pick up).

## 4 pm snack
- If the child slept through 11 am, the 11 am options can be used here.
- **Curd (homemade)** is great at 4 pm — also one of the best **sensory developmental plays** (let the child play/eat/smear it; add beetroot for "red curd"). Even if the family worries curd causes cold/cough, daily curd is fine; the probiotic benefit is a bonus. (Alternate so you don't repeat the morning item — e.g., pulpy fruit at 11 → potato/sweet potato at 4, and vice versa.)
- **Cheese cube/slice** is excellent for **~8–10 months+** (peak teething): supports teeth, **calcium, vitamin D, bones, and tooth enamel**.

## 6 pm snack
- Ideally give the snack around **5:30–6 pm** and take the child **out for a walk**.
- Give **smoothies, porridges, soups** (e.g., teas + soups/smoothies/porridges; the MyDvija book *Baby's Yum Food Diary* has **300+ recipes**) — these keep the child full longer.
- Crispy items that soften when soaked (no choking risk): **makhana** (plain, not flavored — roast in a little ghee + pepper, halve), **churmura** (puffed ragi/wheat/rice), **corn flakes, muesli** (soak a little). Can also be lightly powdered.

## Snacks ≠ full meal
Snacks don't require the cereal-protein-fiber combination, but they're a great place to add **fiber, weight gainers, probiotics, and protein**.

## Ready-made / make-ahead options (good for working mothers)
- Prefer a known **home baker** to make laddus.
- *Yum Food Diary* has **30+ finger foods**: ragi laddu, dry-fruit laddu, dates laddu, fox-nut (makhana) laddu, amaranth/rajgira laddu, plus **baked sticks**.
- A single small laddu can be enough for a snack. **Make laddus, sticks and cookies on weekends** to cover the next 8–10 days; keep **cheese cubes, pulpy fruit** stocked → far more peaceful snack planning.
