# 5.41 — Bonus 4: When to Offer Water to Babies (Age-wise Guide)

## Before 6 months: NO water
- Before 6 months, give **nothing but mother's milk** — **breastmilk is ~80% water + 20% milk**, perfectly balanced so nutrients absorb well.
- The "summer baby is thirsty / sticking out tongue" worry is a **myth** in any season.
- **Why water harms a young baby:** a baby's body is ~**75% water** (vs ~55% in adults / 2-yr-olds), and the **kidneys are too small/immature to filter extra water** for the first 6 months. Extra water → kidney load → **diluted sodium** → hindered **brain development**, irritation, confusion, crying; in extreme cases **water intoxication**.
- *(Cultural Ayurvedic exception: a drop or two of water with ajwain/fennel for gas is okay if you trust that method.)*

## After 6 months (when solids start)
- No proven exact amount, but research suggests a child's total water is ~**1–1.3 L/day**, counting **breastmilk (80% water), formula (~50% water), and food** — so a baby on only breast/formula already gets ~1 L.
- Once solids begin, give **1 sip of water every 1–2 hours** after eating, so the child learns water as a new "taste."
- **~8–10 sips/day is enough** when the child also gets soup, coconut water, juice, smoothie, dal, etc.

## How & what kind of water
- **Use a sippy cup/sipper** in routine (or a spoon to introduce). A good sippy cup has a **soft spout, a no-leak valve** (water comes only when the child sucks), and a **good grip/handle** for independent drinking (~7–8 months).
- **Plain filtered water** from the start. **Don't re-boil-and-cool filtered water** (that double-filters it — unnecessary now that filters exist; boiling-cooling was an old disinfecting method). No filter at home → give cold/boiled-cooled water.
- **No sugar/honey water** (none before 11 months). If the child won't take plain water, occasionally give **diluted fresh juice (1 part juice : 10 parts water)** or **coconut water** — but the child must still know the taste of **plain water**.
- **Temperature:** **room temperature** (not fridge-cold, not heated). Keep it in a **closed/lidded** sipper (hygiene), not open.
- **During meals:** avoid a lot of water mid-meal (fills the small stomach). Prefer water **between meals or after eating** (1–2 sips).

## Out-of-the-box tip: bitter (neem) water
Keep **two sippers** — one plain, one with **3–4 fresh neem leaves soaked** (don't boil). Alternate sometimes; to the child it's just a new taste. Research suggests bitter intake strengthens the **liver and the body's fighting mechanism**. Do this for ~1–2 years, then optionally stop.
