# 5.43 — Bonus 6: Honey — Safe or Not Safe?

## The rule: NO honey before the first birthday
Per **WHO**, do not give even a drop of honey until the child is **1 year old.**

## The science
- Honey can carry the bacterium **Clostridium botulinum**, found in **soil and pollen grains** (the flower center) — exactly where honey is collected, so its spores commonly end up in honey.
- In a baby's gut (large intestine not yet developed), these dormant spores **wake up and grow**, causing a form of **food poisoning (infant botulism)** that can affect the **nervous system and blood circulation** → lethargy, excessive sleepiness, unresponsiveness.
- Adults and **children over 1 year** are fine because the developed large intestine has plenty to digest and the bacteria **don't grow**.
- Cited research: **~8% of children** showed honey-related food poisoning. Reactions appear **8–36 hours** after eating (not immediately).

## Cultural practices that should still wait until 1 year
- Writing on a newborn's tongue with honey (for "sweet speech"), honey to stop **hiccups**, or honey before a **vaccination** to ease pain — all should **wait until after 1 year** despite tradition.

## After 1 year
Honey is fine **in moderation** (e.g., a little drizzled on a pancake) — "too much of anything is bad."

## What to use instead (no sugar, no honey before 1 year)
Natural sweeteners: **dried dates powder, fruit purees, dates paste.** (Sugar is chemically processed — not good for the child now.)

## If honey was given (even after 1 year) and the child seems off
Excessively lethargic / unresponsive / fully sleeping / unusually cranky → **see the doctor** to check for a toxic effect.
