# 5.40 — Bonus 3: Cow / Animal Milk for Babies — When, Which, How Much

## When: not before ~1 year
**Cow / buffalo / goat (animal) milk is a strict no until the child is more than 11 months (~1 year).** Before that, give **breastmilk** (or **formula** where breastmilk isn't available).
- **Myth:** "if you don't start cow milk at 6 months (diluted 50:50 with water), the child will never drink milk." **False.**
- **Why animal milk is unsuitable early:** very **low iron** (a core baby need); **very high protein** that's hard for the baby's intestine to digest; high **potassium/load on the kidneys**.
- **Breastmilk** = the ideal (antibodies, immunity, vitamin D, iron, easily digested protein). **Formula** is the next best (fortified to mimic breastmilk) but is **also not a replacement** for breastmilk — if the baby takes breastmilk, don't switch to formula. **Formula is not compulsory** even for working mothers (manageable with breastmilk) unless medically required.

## Milk vs. milk products
- **Milk products** (curd, cheese, butter) → fine from **6 months** (processing into curd makes them digestible). [Note: an earlier lesson cites cheese from 8 months.]
- **Milk itself** → only **after 1 year**.

## How to give (after 12 months)
- Use a **regular cup / thick textured glass** (like a roadside "cutting-tea" glass) — good grip, jaw development, encourages **independent drinking**.
- **Avoid sippy cups; nipple bottles are a strict no** (never give top-milk in a nipple bottle, especially at night). (Nipple bottle should be stopped by **8–9 months** anyway for jaw development → sippy cup → regular cup/glass after 12 months.)
- **Whole milk** — heat it, cool it, then give (high fat + calcium needed for growth spurts in the first 2 years). For **buffalo milk** (higher magnesium/fat), the instructor removed the **cream layer** after boiling, then gave it.
- **No added sugar / Bournvita / Complan / artificial flavor** (such "nutrition powders" only after 2 years, with doctor's advice). Use **natural flavors** instead — colored/flavored milk with dates paste, roasted-peanut flour (peanut kheer), etc.

## How much & when
- **Upper limit: 600–700 ml/day of milk + milk products combined** (curd, butter, cheese, milk). More milk **displaces other nutritious food** (a real case: a 2-year-old on 1.5 L milk who "wouldn't eat"). **Milk is not a replacement for other foods.**
- **Best time:** with **breakfast** or alongside a **snack** (so it isn't seen as exclusive milk). Breastfed kids may take top-milk slowly (10 ml → 20 ml over days) — fine.
- **Not at bedtime** — risks **bedwetting**, harms **oral health** if not brushed after, and over-drinking causes **constipation**.

## Milk allergy & plant milks
- **Allergy signs after starting milk:** severe constipation, anal bleeding, severe vomiting → talk to the doctor.
- **Soy / almond / coconut milk** are options (almond milk = high vitamin E, but a bit lower protein & vitamin D — pair with regular milk). For **lactose intolerance / milk allergy**, discuss almond milk with the doctor.
