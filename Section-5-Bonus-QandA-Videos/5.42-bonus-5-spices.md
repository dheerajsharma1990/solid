# 5.42 — Bonus 5: Spices to Make Baby Food Tasty (15 spices)

Don't depend on salt for taste. *(Real example: a child who "won't eat without salt" ate well after a parent added roasted **cumin powder**.)* When each spice is allowed and how to use it:

| # | Spice | From | Notes / use |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Cumin (jeera) powder** | 6 mo | Dry-roast cumin, grind fine; 1–2 pinches in food. |
| 2 | **Curry leaves** | 6 mo | In ghee tempering for khichdi/dal; remove the big leaves before feeding — adds taste & aroma. |
| 3 | **Asafoetida (hing)** | 6 mo (scientifically) | Ayurveda: **tamasic** (anger-inducing) — prefer adding **after 1 year**, especially if the parents are short-tempered. |
| 4 | **Cardamom powder** | 6 mo | For sweet dishes (porridge, kheer, halwa). |
| 5 | **Cinnamon powder** | 6 mo | Helps colic & constipation; half-pinch for taste. |
| 6 | **Black pepper powder** | 6 mo | Soups, khichdi, curry. |
| 7 | **Garlic** | (with food) | Cloves in tempering (remove before serving), or paste in porridge/pancake/dosa/idli/khichdi. |
| 8 | **Ginger** (or ginger powder) | (with food) | Aids digestion, relieves gas; powder if the family doesn't use fresh ginger. |
| 9 | **Turmeric** | 6 mo | Superb **probiotic** → strengthens immunity; in khichdi/curry/soup/paratha/omelette/dosa/idli. |
| 10 | **Red chilli powder** | 12–13 mo | Only after ~1 year, mild quantity (food shouldn't be too spicy). |
| 11 | **Green chilli** | after 1 yr | Add to tempering and remove again for a slight taste, not spicy. |
| 12 | **Garam masala** (cloves, bay leaf, etc.) | 12 mo | Before that (7–8 mo+) add whole cloves to tempering and remove — taste without the spice powder. |
| 13 | **Mint** | (6 mo+) | Soups, khichdi, curry. |
| 14 | **Coriander (leaves)** | 6 mo | Soups, khichdi, theplas. |
| 15 | **Yellow fenugreek (methi) seeds** | 10–11 mo (whole) | Earlier: lightly roast, powder, add a pinch. **Bitter → good for liver & cardiovascular health**, and teaches a new taste. |
