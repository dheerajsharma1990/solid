# 5.45 — Bonus 8: Massage & Acupressure for Constipation

*Part 2 of the potty/constipation topic (Part 1 = Bonus 1, color/texture/frequency). Presenter: Shrreya Shah — childbirth educator & **acupressure therapist**. Babies (breast- or bottle-fed) commonly have colic/gas and strain (turn red) while passing stool — usually normal, but these techniques give relief.*

**When:** do the **tummy massage at least once daily** (twice/day for ~8 days if the child struggles more, then back to once). Do **acupressure** ideally daily during the **morning and night massage**, and also any time the child seems in trouble. If the child enjoys/relaxes, continue a bit longer. *(Both massage and acupressure are safe; do them gently, smiling — not stressed.)*

## Massage technique (use oil)
Use a baby massage oil (the instructor uses MyDvija baby oil — turmeric, sandalwood, coconut, essential, almond oils). A few drops normally; more oil if a bath follows.

1. **Clockwise tummy circles** — follows the direction the digestive system moves food. ~**30–60 sec** (to comfort).
2. **"I-Love-You" (ILU) strokes** — **I** (stroke down on the baby's left), **L** (across then down), **U** (up the right, across, and down) — ~**30–60 sec**.
3. **"Open the book"** — both hands sweep outward from the center — stretches the insides, eases colic, smoothens potty.
4. **Cycling the legs** (count 1–10) — helps pass gas and stool — ~30–60 sec.
5. **Gentle hold-and-stretch** of the legs, repeated.

## Acupressure points (no oil needed; circular massage or gentle "tap-tap," ~60 sec each, both hands/feet)
1. **Base of the index finger toward the thumb** (just below the nail) — massage or tap-tap for ~60 sec on each hand (tiny fingers → tap instead of pressing).
2. **Outer edge of the palm (four fingers' area)** — corresponds to the **large intestine** (where stool finally exits) — circular massage or tap-tap ~60 sec, both hands.
3. **Just below the knee, on the outer side of the leg** — supports digestion and reduces stomach cramps — massage with two/three fingers ~60 sec, both legs (slightly up and down too if unsure of the exact point).
4. **The whole heel (and the side of the foot)** — again the "final exit" area — massage/press the entire heel ~60 sec, plus the side portion, both feet.

*Acupressure improves blood circulation and organ function; massage stimulates circulation; touch therapy itself helps. If unsure which suits your child, do both — whichever soothes the child, do longer.*
