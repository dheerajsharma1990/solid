# 3.21 — Can We Offer Salt to the Baby? (Which, How Much, When)

- **No added salt before 1 year.** Mother's milk already contains the salt (sodium) a baby needs.
- **Why:** added salt raises the body's **sodium level**, which unnecessarily **loads the kidneys** and can **affect brain functioning**. Understand that the allowed amount of salt is *already* reaching the child via milk — it's only the **extra** salt we want to add that's not allowed.
- **After 1 year:** you may use **pink salt / rock salt** (lower sodium proportion). The allowed amount for a 1-year-plus child is **~1.1 g (about ½ teaspoon) in 24 hours.**
- **"How will my child eat without salt?"** The child has no concept of "salty food" — everything is new. Build taste with **carom seeds (ajwain), mint**, and other **spices** (a dedicated spices video gives the full list). Don't depend on salt.
- **If the child eats something from your (salted) plate:** no lifetime problem. For a ~9–10-month-old eating a bit off your plate, it's normal/okay. For a very small baby eating something by mistake, don't panic. **But don't add salt to the food you cook for the child** until 1 year.

*Parenting is about confidence, not fear.*
