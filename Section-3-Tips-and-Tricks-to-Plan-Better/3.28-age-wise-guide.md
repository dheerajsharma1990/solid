# 3.28 — Age-Wise Guide for Offering Food (and the Plate)

*A visual showcase of sample baby platters is shown on screen starting at **[00:04:02]** ("you can see some platters") — some mirror the mother's plate, some show tikki items. Refer to the video for the plating visuals.*

## ~6 months (first weeks)
**Two valid approaches** to begin:
- **Approach A (gentler):** 3 days of moong/dal-rice **water** and hand-crushed moong (thick water), then for **8–10 days** simple purees — pomegranate juice, carrot puree, apple puree — before moving to dal-rice + finger food.
- **Approach B (direct):** skip juices/purees and **start directly** with **hand-crushed dal-rice, lightly mashed sabzi, smashed khichdi**.
- **What/how much:** there's no fixed amount — 2–5 spoons or half a bowl, depends on the child. (Food eaten while soaking it in the lap doesn't count.)
- **Foods at 6 months:** dal-rice, khichdi, curd-rice, hand-crushed rice; idli, dosa, uttapam, chilla; start keeping **finger foods** in the plate.

## ~6.5 months — the plate
- Use a **wooden suction plate** with **partitions**: e.g., 2 small paratha pieces in one, finger food in another, dal-rice balls in another.
- Also: any **tikki/pancake/small chilla**, a chutney, homemade sausage; **idli** cut in half and sauteed in ghee; **overnight moong-dal** (with spinach, rice flour, garlic, ginger) made into **waffles** in small pieces.
- *"My baby only drinks 140 ml milk — how will small waffles count as lunch?"* — that's not the comparison; give **what's right for nutrition**.

## ~7 months+
- Add **parathas** to lunch: fenugreek, onion, potato, paneer.
- **Puree vs. not:** a 7-month-old should **not** still be eating smooth puree. There's a real difference between "puree" and properly textured food — e.g., one couple's "dal-rice after puree" turned out to be **smasher-mashed, granular** dal-rice-veg, which is the **right** texture/habit.

## Always ask: what, how, when, why, and in what combination you're feeding.

## If the child eats only 2–3 bites
**Offer the next snack a little early** and give **banana / potato / sweet potato** to fill the stomach. **Give as much food as the child eats** — there's no fixed answer to "how much."
