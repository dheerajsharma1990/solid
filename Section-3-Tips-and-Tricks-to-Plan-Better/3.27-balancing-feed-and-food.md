# 3.27 — How to Balance Breast/Bottle Feed and Food

## Core rules
- **Until 1 year**, breastfeeding is the **primary** nutrition; **after 1 year** it's **secondary**. But at both stages: **milk must never become the child's food.** If you give a full breastfeed/bottle every time the child spits food out, the child learns *"I don't need to eat — milk is coming."*
- **The child must be happily hungry to eat.** Typical pattern: if the child gets hungry ~2.5 hours after a feed, **offer solids ~1.5–2 hours after the feed** — then solids are taken well.
- After the child eats 2–5 bites: if it's breastfeed time, give it; otherwise **re-offer the same meal after a ~½-hour gap** (re-offer *that meal's* fresh food only — don't serve morning food in the evening or last night's leftovers in the morning; cook fresh).

## Bottle & quantity discipline
- **Stop the bottle by 11 months (compulsory).** Even before that, don't let the child drink **1–2 litres of milk** and skip food — a liquid-filled stomach won't eat solids with excitement.
- Don't let the child reach the **over-tired / over-hungry** stage (a hungry child seeing you will demand breastfeed). Managing this keeps feed *and* food balanced. A bath followed by an afternoon sleep slot helps the routine.

## How many feeds per day (guideline)
- **~5 months (pre-solids):** ~**6–10** breast/bottle feeds.
- **Early solids:** day feeds ~**3–5**, night feeds ~**2–3**.
- **8 months+:** train the child to sleep (a **sleep-training course** exists); aim for **2–3 day feeds + 1–2 night feeds**, total ~**3–5**.
- **1 year+:** over 24 hours, ~**2–3 breastfeeds**; **bottle fully closed**, **formula stopped after 11 months**; **cow's milk 1–2 times/day**; **night feed must stop**. If the child isn't breastfeeding, **don't give a bottle at night** — it badly damages the teeth.

## Don't let night become day
If the child drinks milk all night and won't eat in the day (treating night like day), fix it with **sleep training**. Engage the child with **different-texture foods, with excitement**, and **plan ahead with a food calendar.**

## Teething rejection (8 months+)
If a child who ate well suddenly rejects food during **teething**: remember *"the child needs the food, not you."* Stop offering; instead **make crunchy/different-shaped things involving the child**, and **eat in front of the child** — this naturally makes the child want to eat.
