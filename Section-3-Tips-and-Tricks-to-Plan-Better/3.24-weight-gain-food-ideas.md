# 3.24 — Baby Weight-Gain Food Ideas

## Reality check on weight first
- Track against **birth weight**: ~double by 5 months, ~**triple by 1 year**. If weight hasn't moved for ~2 months but is still "moved" relative to birth weight, you're fine — don't compare to other babies.
- **Cheeks**: chubby baby cheeks largely disappear after ~6 months as height increases and the face lengthens — the child looking a bit thin is normal.
- **Ghee ≠ weight.** Feeding lots of ghee adds **fat**, not weight. **Weight means proper muscle mass**, not fat. What matters is the child being in the **fit range**.

## Top weight-gain foods (also work as fillers / high-calorie nutritious snacks)
These increase weight, keep the child full, and can be given in the next snack if a meal was light:

1. **Egg** (top choice) — white and yolk; usually safe **before 7–8 months** (introduce per the allergen guidance in lesson 2.13). Vegetarians have plenty of other options.
2. **Cheese** — from the **8th month**; great for **dental health, no cavities, protein**.
3. **Banana** — all seasons, any variety (small/cardamom or big). The "banana causes cold" belief is largely a belief system — give it.
4. **Potato** — boil and saute in butter; or roll in semolina/rava for crispiness; or make raw-banana/potato "french fries" with a little rice flour.
5. **Sweet potato** — sweet-potato paratha at 11 am, or in 4 pm snack, or in 6 pm soup.
6. **Avocado** — as a spread, finger food, or in soups. Very helpful.
7. **Lentils & whole grains** — green/yellow/black moong, masoor — all allowed within the first year; good weight gainers.
8. **Milk & milk products** — paneer, **curd** (cow/buffalo) allowed within the year; **milk itself only after 1 year**.
9. **Olive oil** — add a little (ideally after **7–8 months**): anti-inflammatory, good for digestion, a good weight gainer, adds flavor.
10. **Non-veg** (chicken, fish) — increases the fat component → weight gain. **Non-veg stock from the 6th month**, proper non-veg **after the 7th month**.

**Fruits also help weight gain** — pear, apple, grapes, mango, and all fruits (100% fine).

**Mindset:** the baby's weight is natural, not a source of stress. Avoid "you're so fat / so thin" comments — a **fit** child is enough.
