# 3.23 — No Teeth: Can My Baby Eat Without Them?

**Yes, 100% — a toothless baby can do BLW / independent eating.**

- **Teething timeline (per pediatric dentists):** first teeth usually **4–6 months**; **delayed teething** can mean teeth around **11 months**; **extremely delayed** is possible — the instructor's own child got the first tooth at **22 months**. Don't stress.
- **Teeth are not used to chew.** Children (and adults) chew with the **jaw, gums, and cheeks**. Chewing/munching matters because it:
  - mixes **saliva** into the food,
  - builds the chewing habit,
  - improves **nutritional absorption**, and
  - protects the **gums and emerging new teeth**.
- **Teeth don't decide what food you give.** Once the basic meal is set, give a toothless baby **parathas, chilla, idli, pancake, waffle, laddu**, etc.
- **Don't wait on teeth** to make the child an independent eater.
