# 3.29 — Seasonal Diet/Food Changes for Kids

**Core principle:** give **seasonal foods** — fruits/vegetables grown in that season give the **highest nutrition**, are most affordable, and are easiest to digest, and they protect against that season's illnesses.

## Summer
- **Avoid:** cold foods — ice cream, cola/Pepsi, cold lassi, cold curd, very cold water (cold + heat clash). Avoid spicy food, biryani/pulao with whole spices (raises acidity), heavy fried food, heavy chicken/mutton, lots of paneer/tofu late at night.
- **Give:** **seasonal fruits** — mango, all **berries** (blueberry, strawberry), watermelon, muskmelon, grapes; **buttermilk / dried butter** (doesn't heat the body the way curd does); **ragi/nachni** (all-season, ideally **sprouted** — cools in summer, warms in winter); keep the child **hydrated** with coconut water, coconut syrup, lemon syrup, **coconut cream**; lots of fruits; basic easy-to-digest food.

## Rainy season
- **Avoid:** **stale food, very spicy food, anything left open or cooked-and-kept all day** (high contamination risk); too many **leafy vegetables** (can cause loose motions).
- **Give:** **buttermilk**; all-season fruits (apple, pear, guava); **oranges** (best in rain & winter); seasonal fruits — jamun, plums, pomegranate, watermelon, cherries; gourd-family vegetables (dudhi/bottle gourd, red pumpkin) and carrot. Maintaining **immunity** is critical (infections spread fast).

## Winter
- **Give heavy-to-digest foods:** urad-dal laddu, flour **sheera**, almond sheera, **dry fruits** — the **best season for weight gain** and traditional homemade sweets. Buttermilk still good.
- **Good winter fruits:** chiku, guava, all-season apple/pear, dragon fruit, kiwi.

## Always
- Match food to the child's **digestion capacity**; adjust when the child is **sick**; during **teething** give **crunchy, colorful** things so the child eats easily.
