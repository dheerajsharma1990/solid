# 3.26 — Ghee & Oil: Which is Safe?

## Ghee
- **From the 6th month.** Use **homemade butter (white butter)** or **homemade ghee** (cow's or buffalo's).
- **Cow's ghee is ideally better** (its fat composition is easier to digest), but if you only have good-quality buffalo ghee at home, that's fine.
- **No market/packaged butter** for ~1 year — high salt, highly processed.

## Oil
- **No refined (machine-made) oil for ~1 year.**
- Best for children: **cold-pressed oil** (traditionally seeds/nuts pressed in wooden churns, no chemicals, pure, nutrient- and vitamin-rich) or cold-pressed/"cow" oils.
- Allowed oils: **almond, coconut, groundnut, sunflower, olive, "brown" oil**, etc.
- **Start any oil in low quantity**, then gradually increase.
- **Cold-pressed oil doesn't store long** — buy small amounts and use quickly.

## How to use them for benefit
- **Ghee in tempering gives little benefit to the child.** To get the benefit, **drizzle ghee on top** of the served paratha/roti/rice/dal-rice — that's very effective.
- **Oil is best used in tempering/cooking** — e.g., 2 drops of olive oil on top of a soup, or sautéing broccoli in olive oil (see lesson 3.25).

*"Healthy kitchen, healthy kids."* Feeding is an art — do it with confidence, and keep sharing your experiences.
