# 3.22 — Tricks to Make Baby Food Sweet Without Sugar

- **No sugar before 2 years.** (Many parents add sugar to formula thinking the baby likes sweet because breastmilk is sweet — don't.)
- **Why no sugar:**
  - Breastmilk has **natural sugar** the body digests easily.
  - Extra sugar within the first year forces the body to release more **insulin** (which controls blood sugar). More insulin → the child becomes **lethargic, lazy, less active, and milestones may be delayed** — all negative.
  - If you make the child used to sweet (because breastmilk is sweet) and keep giving sweet food, the child will start **rejecting other tastes**.
- **Also no honey, no excess jaggery, no corn sugar** in this period.
- **What to give instead (1 year onward you can begin jaggery/honey):** within the first year, expose the child to **all different tastes** — **bitter** food, **soft/plain** food without added taste, and **umami** foods like **curd, paneer, broccoli**. Give the full range of tastes, just **not added sugar**.
