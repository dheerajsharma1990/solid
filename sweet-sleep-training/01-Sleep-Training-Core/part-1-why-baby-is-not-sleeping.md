# Sleep Training — Part 1: Understand WHY the Baby Isn't Sleeping

## What sleep training is (and the mindset)
- Humans evolved on **circadian rhythm** (sun up → activity, dark → sleep). Sleep is a **physical need of the body** — like food and parental touch — not an emotional one. A baby crying at night is giving a **physical cry** (for sleep / nipple), not an emotional one.
- Managing the child's three core needs — **food, sleep, and (parental) touch** — is the parent's core responsibility.
- **Do it with respect.** Sleep training done out of your own irritation/worry (crossing the "respect line") will not work.
- *Presenter credentials: childbirth educator; certified lactation/breastfeeding consultant; master's in nutrition; certified child play therapist; certified child sleep therapist; certified acupressure therapist; ~17 years' experience; works with 7 lakh+ families.*

## Resolve these FIRST — delay sleep training until fixed
1. **Poor feeding/eating** — if the child barely eats/drinks and is cranky all day, see a child nutritionist (weaning plan for babies, eating plan for toddlers).
2. **Breathing issues** — **mouth breathing or snoring** at night must be fixed *immediately* via a **pediatric orthodontist + ENT**; the body can't sleep-train until this stops.
3. **Weight gain off track** — birth weight should **double by ~5 months, triple by the 1st birthday**, then gain **~500 g–2 kg/year** in years 1→2 and 2→3. If not, consult a nutritionist/pediatrician.
4. **Nutritional deficiency** — pale, dull, dark circles, low energy, frequently sick → check **iron, vitamin D, vitamin C** (iron/vitamin-D deficiency disrupts both eating and sleep). Confirm by appearance or blood test.
5. **Recent infection/illness** — let the child fully recover (allopathy/homeopathy) before training.
6. **Lack of clarity on what/when to feed** — rough guide: ~6 mo → 1 meal; ~7 mo → at least 1 meal; ~8 mo → 3 meals + 2 snacks; ~1 yr → eats from the **family pot**. Get this right first.

## The big truth: **more parental involvement = more disturbed sleep**
Rocking/feeding the baby back to sleep every time *increases* night waking. (Restating: a night cry is a **physical** cry for sleep/nipple, not emotional.)

## "Safe sleep" = A-B-C-D-E
- **A — Alone:** the child sleeps in their **own separate space** (crib, palana, jhula, bed-trolley, or own mattress). **Co-sleeping (same room) is fine; bed-sharing should be avoided.**
- **B — Back:** sleep on the back until neck control; after **3 months** the baby may sleep on the stomach/side.
- **C — Cot (close, separate sleeping space).**
- **D — Defined night surrounding:** the child clearly knows *this is my sleep time/place/environment*.
- **E — Eternally peaceful parents:** a panicked/disturbed parent shows up directly in the child's sleep.
- **Not safe:** loose blankets, pillows, many toys on the bed, bed-sharing, a 24×7 pacifier. (Same room = okay.)

## Why the child wakes — the three "E"s
**Environment, Eating, Emotions** (emotions ↔ hormones are deeply linked). Expanded into specific causes below.

### 1. Non-appropriate ENVIRONMENT
- **Bright light** (natural or artificial — tube lights, street lamp, open windows).
- **Blue light** — any screen/mobile light in the room raises over the eyeballs and blocks sleep (even a little mobile glow counts).
- **Loudness** — noisy play near sleep time.
- **Active play/movement** near bedtime (over-stimulating to "tire" the child) backfires.

### 2. Poor sleep hygiene / naps
- **Inconsistent bed/wake times** → "poor brain wiring," difficulty distinguishing day/night.
- **Wrong number/gap/duration/method of naps** for the age → over- or under-tired child.
- **No bedtime ritual** → the mind never wires "these things mean I sleep now."
- **Wrong day activities / wake-up window** between naps.

### 3. Hunger & the food–hormone connection
- **Myth: "more food = more sleep."** False. Stuffing formula until the child passes out doesn't help.
- **Fill the day's food/water/feed needs during the day** so the child doesn't wake to feed at night. Don't skip daytime feeds or let the child sleep too long without feeding — under-eating by day → frequent night feeding.
- **Carbs/sweets at night are the enemy of sleep:** sweet/heavy dinner (lapsi, dalia, sheera, porridge) → **high insulin → high cortisol (stress hormone) → low melatonin (sleep hormone) → disrupted sleep.**
- **Fix:** **dinner at 7–7:30**, **protein- and fat-heavy, stomach-filling, NOT sweet** → low insulin → low cortisol → more melatonin → peaceful sleep. ("Getting sleepy" vs "feeling sleepy" are different — melatonin is what makes you *feel* it.)

### External sleep associations ("addictions")
Whatever the child needs *to fall asleep* becomes a crutch: **breastfeeding, bottle, nipple/pacifier in mouth, rocking, swinging, car rides, lap-carrying, swaddling.**
- **Sleep-cycle mechanics:** alert → shallow → deep → REM/shallow, repeating. At each cycle transition the child surfaces and asks *"where's my nipple/bottle?"* If they only know how to fall asleep *with* your help, they wake fully every cycle. The goal: **teach the child to move from one cycle to the next on their own.**
- **Bottle must be stopped by 11 months.**

## 12 factors for better sleep
**Gut health** (curd, buttermilk, yogurt, broccoli, kiwi, guava, vitamin-C foods) · **Hormones** (dark → melatonin) · **Exercise** (no shortcuts) · **Nutrition** (most crucial) · **Emotional health** (of mother *and* child) · **Developmental play** (cognitive/social/outdoor/garden) · **Happiness** (a happy parent → a peaceful child) · **Consistency** (same bedtime, wake time, routine) · **Routine** (how the day starts and ends) · **Quality time** (3 key windows: 10 min after waking, 10 min before sleep, and — for working parents — 10 min before/after coming home; this dissolves "separation anxiety") · **Massage** (bedtime massage) · **Avoiding addictions**.

## Getting the environment right (the practicals)
- **Darkness:** the room should be so dark you **can't read a book in it** — *a darkness gradient (rooms numbered 1–10) is shown on screen around* **[00:30:00]**; aim for the **8–9–10** level of darkness for **both day and night** sleep. Use **blackout paper (Amazon)** or aluminium foil + black paper on the window.
  - **Myth:** "child is scared of the dark" — false; the child spent 9 months in the dark. Turning on a light makes the body melatonin-poor and blocks sleep.
  - **No night lamp.** If you must, a tiny **red light** in a corner only. **No light through the night** — do diaper changes etc. in the dark during the training phase.
- **Shift bedtime earlier gradually:** if the child currently sleeps at 12, start the routine at **11:30**, then move ~30 min earlier every 2–3 days.
- **No active talking or even whispering** in the room (whisper disturbs sleep too).
- **White noise:** a machine is fine, but **chanting, string instruments, or gentle humming** work well.
- **Clothing & temperature:**
  - Use a **sleeping bag/sack (~₹700, photo shown ~[00:33:55])** — hands free, feet inside, bottom zip for diaper changes. **Avoid swaddling after 3 months.** Cotton/muslin, age-appropriate, not tight/loose.
  - **Bamboo/eco diapers** (regular diapers take ~450 yrs to biodegrade; bamboo are breathable, less disruptive). **Stop diapers by 18 months.**
  - **No socks/mittens/cap 24×7** — hands and feet release the body's heat; socks all day trap it. Cotton/muslin only; a cap only makes sense if it actually covers the ears.
  - **AC allowed from day 1** at **23–27 °C** (sweet spot **23–26 °C**); **fan allowed** to circulate cool air. Check the neck — slight moisture/sweat means the temperature isn't comfortable.

## Bedtime routine — the fixed 4-step sequence (same order, every night)
1. **Feed** — breast / bottle / cow-milk first (the first feed after waking, and before each nap). Let the child sit up after feeding.
2. **Sleep hygiene** — bath/brush/washroom, pyjamas, diaper.
3. **Connecting** — bedtime massage, stretches, hug, smiley ball, stories/reading, prayers/meditation, slow-stimulation activity (touch, stroke, yoga, chanting).
4. **Good night / bedtime** — music/humming/chanting, **dark room**, lullaby, soothing into the sleeping bag.
- **Sequence and nightly consistency are non-negotiable.** Put the child down **awake (not already drowsy/napping)** and let them fall asleep there. *(A washroom break is announced after this slide, ~[00:38:00].)*
