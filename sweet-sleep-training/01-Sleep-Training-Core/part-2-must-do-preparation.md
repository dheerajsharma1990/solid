# Sleep Training — Part 2: Must-Do Preparation

*This is the longest, most practical session. Several **age-wise charts are shown on screen** (nap reference ~[00:15:42], feed/food chart ~[00:29:39]) and an **acupressure demo at [00:02:02]** — the customized numbers also live in the course **"Material"** notes tab.*

## Recap: the fixed bedtime sequence (same order, every night, 15–25 min)
**Feeding → Hygiene (bath/brush/washroom/pyjama/diaper) → Connecting (massage, stretches, hug, reading) → Good night (chanting, lights off, onto the bed).** If you currently feed *last* and the baby falls asleep on the feed, that routine cannot sleep-train — the feed must move earlier (see "weaning the feed-to-sleep habit" below).

## Planning tools for the "connecting" step
- **Acupressure (3 points, demoed at [00:02:02]):** (1) **centre of the foot** (solar-plexus point) — press gently; (2) the point **below the outer side of the hand**; (3) the point **between the thumb and index finger**. Can be done anytime, ideal during the bedtime massage.
- **Occupational therapy:** cradle the baby like the old "doll in a dupatta," hold and gently sway while singing ("la la la"); rub a little oil on your belly. (Don't put the baby to sleep *in* a swing.)
- **Aura cleanser = a bedtime bath** (science-backed). If a once-a-day bath, do it in the **evening**. The "night bath causes cough" belief is a myth; in very cold/snowy places, dip the baby to the shoulders in warm water and wipe, or sponge with warm water.
- **Warm-oil forehead massage** — gentle strokes on the forehead help the baby sleep well.
- **Yellow smiley-ball massage** — roll a soft yellow ball gently over the feet/belly.

## Bedtime ritual — sequence differs under 1 yr vs over 1 yr
- **Under 1 year:** breast/bottle feed (fill the stomach) → after 10–15 min, bedtime massage/stretches → reading/occupational → gum & tongue cleaning with soft cloth → gentle moisturizer (calming, not rushed) → diaper + pyjama/night dress → **sleeping bag** → place in the **private sleep space**. Steps 1–11 in **dim light**; the **final step = lights fully off, completely dark**; then "good night."
- **Over 1 year:** **no formula/bottle** (breastfeed only if continuing, ideally to 18 mo–2 yr) → bath → potty seat (get them used to it) → brushing & tongue cleaning → night dress → diaper (**stop diapers after 18 months**) → reading → prayer → hug → music/chant → **lights off (completely dark)**. Steps 1–9 dim, step 10 = dark.

## Weaning the "feed-to-sleep / nipple-in-mouth" habit (the million-dollar question)
Root cause: the baby only knows how to fall asleep *with* the nipple, so at each sleep-cycle transition they wake and demand it. Fixes:
1. **Gradually shift the feed UP the routine** — move the feed earlier by ~2 steps every 1–2 days, so over **~4 days** the feed is no longer the last thing before sleep.
2. **Caregiver switch** — mother feeds, then hands the child to husband/grandparent/nanny for the rest of the calming routine (in dim light); the mother preferably leaves the room (so the child doesn't keep demanding breast).
3. **Keep the baby AWAKE during feeding** — the baby must not fall asleep on the breast/bottle. (Hard, but essential.)
4. **One firm person in the room** who can handle the baby's cry without rocking it into a game. Only **one** person in the room.
5. **Consistency for 7 days** is the key.

## Age-wise nap reference (shown on the chart)
For each age there's a defined **number of naps**, **wake-up window** (awake time between naps), **nap duration**, and a **nap cut-off time** (after which no daytime nap is allowed):
- e.g. **3 months → 4–5 naps; 5 months → 3–4 naps; 7 months → 2 naps; ~13 months → 1 nap.**
- **Cut-off examples:** a 5-month-old must not nap after **~5:30 pm**; a 10-month-old not after **~4:30 pm** — then sleep directly at night (~8:30).
- **Total sleep:** ~**10–11 hours at night** + the rest made up in day naps (24-hr total ~**12–16 hours**, age-dependent).
- **Ideal bedtime ~8:30** (routine starts 8:00, lights off 8:30).

## Sleep pressure (the "tub" analogy, ~[00:19:26])
Awake time fills a "tub"; a nap empties it. **Forcing the baby to stay awake too long over-fills the tub** → stress hormones rise → **overtired** child who paradoxically *can't* sleep, is hyperactive/cranky, and wakes all night.
- **Overtired signs:** too playful/hyperactive at bedtime, wakes crying after a short nap, multiple night wakings, sleeps only 20–30 min in the day, "habit cry" (0→10 instantly).
- **Undertired signs:** sleeps too much/too long in the day (e.g. from too much formula), bored, low energy, **split nights** (sleeps 8–11, awake 11–3, sleeps again), early-morning waking. Caused by too *little* sleep pressure.
- **The fix is the right wake-up window for the age** — neither over- nor under-tired.

## Habit cry vs hunger cry (how to tell)
- **Habit:** cries **0→10 instantly/loudly with irritation**; cries even *after* a feed; just wants to munch/keep the nipple in the mouth; wakes every 1.5–2 hrs (a 4-month-old waking that often = habit; a 3-month-old can go **3–5 hrs** without a feed).
- **Hunger:** **soft cry that gradually builds**; goes quiet and peaceful once fed; usually after a **long stretch (4–6 hrs)**; drinks a real quantity over a real duration.
- **Allowed night feeds:** under 4 mo — fine to feed; **4–8 mo — once (max twice); after 8 mo — one or zero.**

## Daytime-nap mistakes to avoid
**Dream feeding** (sneaking a bottle to extend sleep), **over-swaddling** (and no swaddle after 3 months), **letting naps run too long** (cap at age-appropriate duration), **skip-feeding by day** (a child who "won't eat lunch" but drinks 300–400 ml milk *is* taking lunch as milk → under-eats calories by day → wakes to feed at night), **too much swinging** (creates a motion addiction), and **all-day diapers** (going diaper-free in the day keeps the child pee-aware and self-trains).

## Morning & night anchors (follow religiously for 14 days)
- **Wake 6–8 am; no sleep after 8 am.** If the child wakes at 9, pull it back ~30 min/day (9:45 → 8:30 → 8:15 → 8 am). Within ~30 min of waking, get the child into **sunlight/fresh light (8–9 am)** → melatonin drops, child is alert.
- **Night sleep between 7–9 pm (ideal 7:30–8:30); lights off by bedtime.** Routine starts ~8.
- **No blue light after 8 pm** — no mobile/screen/monitor in the room at all (not even a parent working in the corner).

## Feed & food age-wise chart (24-hr feeds, day/night split, meals, weight/week)
- **3 mo:** 8–10 feeds/24h (≈6–7 day + 2 night), no solids, +150 g/wk.
- **3–6 mo:** 6–8 feeds (6 day + 2 night, or 5+2), no solids, +130 g/wk.
- **6 mo:** 4–6 feeds (2 night + 2–4 day), 1 meal, +110 g/wk.
- **7–8 mo:** 4–5 feeds (shorter), 2–3 meals + 1 snack, +100 g/wk (≈2 night feeds counted).
- **9–12 mo:** total ~3 feeds, 3 meals + 1–2 snacks, +90–100 g/wk; **weight triples by 1st birthday.**
- **1–2 yr:** 1–3 feeds/24h (e.g. bedtime + morning, none at night), **eats from the family pot** (3 meals = cereal+protein+fiber + 2 snacks), +500 g–2 kg/yr.

## The FIFA wake-window formula (~[00:45:30])
Wake-window pattern through the day by age:
- **Under 5 months (incl. <3 mo):** **2 / 2.5 / 2** hours.
- **5 months–1 year:** **3 / 3.5** hours.
- **Over 1 year:** **4 / 4.5 / 4** hours.
After each wake window → a nap (age-appropriate length, **in a dark room** — curtains/foil/blackout). Do this for **21 days** and the child sleeps anywhere.

## Sample day schedules (assuming ~7:30 am wake)
- **3–6 months (3 naps, FIFA 2/2.5/2):** wake 7:30 → feed → active play → ~9:15 sun bath → 9:15–9:30 nap routine → **nap 9:30–11** → feed 11:15 → bath/massage 11:30–12:15 → feed 12:15 (awake) → active play (fresh air) → 1:15 nap routine → **nap 1:30–2:30** → **nap 4:30–5:30** → bedtime routine 7:30 → bed 8.
- **7–8 months (2 naps + 2 meals + optional snack):** wake 7:30 → feed 7:45 → active play 8–9:30 (gap before food) → breakfast 9:30 → bath/massage/sun 10–11:15 → nap routine → **nap 11:30–12:30** → lunch 1–1:30 (after freshening, not on waking) → sensory play → feed 2:45–3 → play → 3:45 nap routine → **nap 4–5:30** → bedtime 8.
- **1 year+ (1 nap, FIFA 4/4.5/4):** wake 7:30 → half-cup cow milk/breastfeed → active play 8–9 → breakfast 9 → bath/massage/sun 9:30–11 → activity → lunch 12 → 12:30 nap routine → **nap 12:45–2:45** → … → bedtime 8. No bottle/formula/night feed after 1 year (one optional night feed ~5 hrs after bedtime if you really want); 3 family-pot meals.

## The mother's own preparation
- **No mobile after 8 pm** (the baby shares the mother's nervous system); calm, dark bedtime.
- **Exercise** (good maternal hormones → better sleep training), **DR (diastasis recti)/back corrections**, daily **meditation/pranayama/breathing**.
- **Early dinner by 7:30, rich in protein + fat, NOT sweet/cereal** — egg white/omelette, paneer/paneer paratha, dal-heavy khichdi, soy keema/chunks, sprout chilla/dosa/paratha; no sweets. If hungry at bedtime, **cow's milk with shatavari** only.
- **Kangaroo care (KCT):** hold the baby skin-to-skin on your chest and dance, twice a day.

## Challenges & their fixes
- **Long naps:** wake the child **on time** (gently) — no over-long day naps; if disturbed, shift by 15 min/day.
- **Dozing off on the breast/bottle:** keep the baby awake — scratch in front of the ear/jaw, gently blow on the forehead/face, keep talking, offer a **rattle/wrist rattle**, sing or tell a story (not a lullaby). Don't wake a sleeping baby just to burp (burping isn't essential after 3 months).
- **Wonder weeks (regression/leap/teething) — the "table of 3":** at **3, 6, 9, 12 months** (and years). Keep the schedule **at least up to the first nap**; last nap and lights-off time stay fixed; the child returns to routine in **2–3 days**; prioritize quality time.
- **Odd-time early waking (4–5:30 am):** **no lights/curtains/screen before 6 am** — let the child play on the bed; still wake at the fixed 7:30, don't let them oversleep (it ruins the first nap).
- **Separation anxiety / working parents:** it's about **connection, not duration** — nail **3 windows**: 10 min after waking, 10 min on returning home (no screen first), 10 min before bed → no separation anxiety.
- **Activities for an only caregiver:** chanting, smiley-ball/acupressure, baby yoga (gentle exercises/stretches), sensory/social/outdoor play, reading.

## Reducing night feeds — the gradual 7-night plan
- **Find the child's "personal best"** (longest-ever night stretch, say 3–3.5 hrs) → don't feed before that.
- **Push the gap +1 hr every 2 days:** nights 1–2 feed only after 3 hrs (e.g. bed 8 → feeds 11, 2, 5); nights 3–4 after 4 hrs (feeds 12, 4); nights 5–6 after 5 hrs (one feed ~1 am; a 6 am wake counts as a day feed); **night 7 = no night feed.**
- **Or reduce feed duration:** 15 → 10 → 7 → 5 → 3 min (cut every 2 days), then stop.
- **Bottle:** reduce the *number* the same way, or reduce volume **90 → 70 → 40 → 0 ml** across nights 1/3/5/7.

## Common confusions (reassurance)
Weight gain **won't** be affected (if the day feed/food schedule is followed); **no dehydration**; if night milk supply drops that's **normal** and won't affect daytime; **no detachment** if the 3 connection windows are kept; working mothers pump for the daytime bottle (and maybe once at night if the partner gives a feed). **Stop the bottle completely** via the gradual reduction; stop motion/swing addictions by putting the child down on the bed.

## The 7-day cry curve (expected)
**Day 1 — most crying; Day 2 — less; Day 3 — even less; Day 4 — much better; Days 5–6 — a spike (lots of crying again); Day 7 — settles into trained sleep.** This is why the 7-day commitment matters.

## "Silly mama" technique
For a very stubborn/crying toddler: enter and act a little forgetful/silly ("oh, mama forgot something in the kitchen, mama will be back") to gently teach that crying at bedtime won't win back the mother's constant presence — without it becoming a confrontation.
