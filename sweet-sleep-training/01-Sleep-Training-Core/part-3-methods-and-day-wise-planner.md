# Sleep Training — Part 3: Methods & Day-Wise Planner

## What sleep training actually is
**Teaching the child to fall asleep by himself** — giving the child the *opportunity* to sleep without a parent's help — and to sleep a **long stretch at night** (3/4/5 hrs → feed if needed → another long stretch).
- **Timeline:** **night sleep settles in 3–7 nights.** **Daytime naps take longer — at least ~3 weeks.** So focus effort on the night; the day follows.

## The 5 methods (ordered by how much parental support they involve)
1. **La Pause (France) — for the first ~3 months.** Don't reward a cry with the nipple. When the baby cries at night, **wait ~10 minutes**; just touch the chest and say "shhh"; or let the baby **cry in your arms 30–40 sec**, then put them back down once calm. (Requires the full daytime + bedtime routine in place.)
2. **Pick-up / put-down — ❌ FAIL method, don't use.** (Pick up when crying, put down when calm, repeat.) Low success, can take months.
3. **Chair / Stay-in-Room / "Parking" method — ✅ brilliant.** One parent stays in the same room while the child falls asleep.
4. **Leave-and-Check — ✅.** Child is alone in a separate, enclosed safe space; you leave and come back to check at intervals. **This is NOT cry-it-out** (in cry-it-out you never return).
5. **Cry-it-out — not taught here.** The instructor's certification is **gentle, peaceful sleep training**, so methods 3 and 4 are the recommended ones.

> **Never reward crying** (no picking up, rocking, or feeding in response to a cry) — it confuses the child into crying repeatedly. **Consistency is respect and care.** The person in the room must be **cry-immune / calm** (a panicking parent raises the child's cortisol → no sleep). It's a **physical need/skill to learn, not emotional torture** — don't turn it into a power struggle.

## Do NOT start sleep training if:
Any alarming sign (weight/eating/infection/illness), **travel within the 21 days**, a **location change/move**, a **recent vaccination (delay 2 weeks)**, or the child is in a **recovery phase** (e.g. just over loose motions).

## Pre-start "compulsive checklist"
1. No alarming signs · 2. Sleep environment set (room dark at the 8/9/10 level) · 3. Pre-sleep routine done · 4. Sleep schedule followed (wake time, bedtime, nap count, gaps) · 5. Feed sequence high in the routine (FIFA) — i.e. the feed moved up, not last.

## When/what to start
- **Under ~8 months:** start **night** immediately; add **day naps** after 1–2 days.
- **8 months+:** start **both night and day** immediately.
- **Start on a Friday** (Fri/Sat nights are hardest, Sunday better, partner back to office Monday).
- **Joint family:** involve **only the primary, non-interfering caregiver**; inform others (prayer/support is fine) but don't let them undo the method.

## Managing night wake-ups
- **Non-feed wake-up:** wait **at least 10 minutes** before responding (a 0→10 instant cry = habit). Then a **30-second** check — don't pick up; check diaper, touch, "shhh, mama/papa is here, it's sleep time," leave. **Repeat every 15 minutes** until asleep. You're present in the room but not engaging.
- **Feed wake-up:** **extend the first feed to the child's personal-best stretch** (3/4/5 hrs) — don't feed before that. La Pause 2–3 min, then **feed keeping the baby awake**, burp, keep awake, back to the method. **Dad managing the night feed is ideal** (he follows the same timing; can give a bottle and wean it down per the guide).
- **Only one parent in the room** (shuffle on alternate days). If there's a breastfeed association, having **husband/grandparent/nanny** put the child down can help — but they must follow the method.

## Pro tips
- **Nap chat:** whatever you **whisper near the child's ear in the first ~15 minutes of sleep becomes reality** — use gentle, calming affirmations ("you sleep so peacefully, mama will feed you a little later").
- **Self-soothing is allowed** — if the child sucks a finger, let them (even help). It teaches "I can sleep on my own"; it won't become a bad habit.

## Age-wise method guide
- **0–3 months:** La Pause; don't let the child sleep on the nipple. By the **4th month** the child should be trained to **5–6 hrs ×2** in 24 hrs.
- **3–6 months:** chair / leave-and-check; night settles fast, **6–7 hrs** at a stretch without feed by day 7.
- **6–8 months:** at least **5 hrs** at a stretch before a feed.
- **8 months–1 year & 1 year+:** **no night feed.**

## Overall timeline
- **Day 1–3:** check alarming signs; introduce the sleep routine.
- **Day 2–4:** establish sleep/feed/food/wake/bedtime; crying but settles fast.
- **Day 3–5:** **stop night feeding** (8-month-old: your choice of 0–1 feed).
- **Day 6–14:** the success story begins (hence the 14-day journey).

## Chair method — day by day
- **Day 1–3:** sit/sleep **beside** the baby (avoid co-sleeping), no talking. Every **5 minutes**, a **30-sec** touch/pat/comforting word; repeat until asleep. **No picking up before 20 min** (if crying hard, you may lift for **max 1 min** then put down — but lifting usually worsens it). If the child gets up and crawls around, try to resettle 1–2×, else let them be in their space (dark, no response).
- **Day 4–6:** sit a little **further away**, no tapping; check every **10 minutes**.
- **Day 7–9:** be in the **far corner**; check only every **20–25 min** (usually not needed).

## Leave-and-check — day by day
- **Day 1–3:** **no one in the room** (baby alone in crib/enclosed bed); routine done, put baby down **awake**, blackout, "good night." Return after **5 minutes** for a 30-sec pat/reassure, leave; repeat every 5 min (stretch to 15 if the child copes).
- **Day 4–6:** check after **15 minutes** each time (→ 20–25 if soothing well).
- **Day 7–9:** check only after **30 minutes** (usually unnecessary).

## Chair vs Leave-and-check (which to pick)
- **Chair = slower** (parent present → some interference/confusion risk), **best for 1 year+** kids (who can walk to your room) and parents with separation anxiety; good for a separate space.
- **Leave-and-check = faster** (settling by ~day 5), **best for under-1-year** kids in a separate space; expect a **high-pitch cry on nights 1–2.**

## What success looks like
The child sleeps a **longer stretch — at least 4 hrs by ~day 6** — and develops **non-dependency on the nipple** (the biggest victory). Bonus: better growth & immunity; **parents sleep far more peacefully by day 7**, and so does the child.

## Common traps
- **Afraid to start** — just start.
- **"Helping" actually confuses** — if you give in after 30 min of crying on night 1, night 2 they'll cry *longer* expecting the same.
- **Don't worry about day naps** — they settle later; just keep the routine.
- **Extinction burst** — crying drops by day 3–4, then **spikes on day 5–6** (the peak before it resolves) — normal; **don't break consistency.**
- **Joint-family trap** — don't let "the kids already wake up, what will you achieve" talk derail you.
- **No partner?** — do it solo; be in the room and see it through.

## What success depends on
**Regularity, love, routine, relaxation** (deep breaths/prayer; remind yourself it's a *physical* need, not emotional torture). *(The timeline is drawn from 10+ research papers; the instructor is mid-certification as a sleep-trainer therapist with 17 yrs of counselling.)*
