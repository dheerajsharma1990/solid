# Bonus Q&A — Baby Waking for Bottle / How to Stop the Bottle Peacefully

*"A 1-year-plus baby still takes a bottle 3–4× a night."*

- **Within 1 year:** stop the bottle gradually — either **drop one bottle at a time**, or **reduce the milk quantity** per bottle.
- **The bottle must be stopped at 11 months** (the "first-birthday gift"). If the child is 1.5–2.5 yrs and still waking for the bottle, that's just a **nipple-to-mouth habit** — stop the bottle **now**, wherever you are. (Why are we in a sleep-training class otherwise?)

## Method 1 — the "Bottle Fairy" (story)
- Find the child's favorite character (Tiger, Chhota Bheem, butterfly, duck).
- **Mark all the bottles, cut off the nipples**, show the child "all our bottles are spoiled." Put them in a bag, keep it **outside for the "bottle fairy"** who'll bring new ones.
- Check with the child a couple of times ("did the fairy take them?"). Later, secretly **throw the old bottles** and leave **two new ones — an open cup and a straw bottle** — outside. Walk out with the child to "discover" them ("the fairy knew you've grown up!").
- The child won't sleep for ~2 days, **sleeps by day 3**, and is **psychologically peaceful** ("the fairy gave me a new bottle"). **No old bottle may surface anywhere in the house.**
- Then **only give water** in the new cup at night (nothing else); resettle with shushing/patting, **not rocking**.

## Method 2 — the goodbye ritual
- Tell the child for **4 days** that the bottle ends (ideally **close it on a Friday**). On the day, put all bottles in a bag and **throw them away together** (orphanage / dustbin / give away — your choice) with the child saying "bye-bye, no more bottles."
- The child **may cry up to ~4.5 hours** — that's normal; sit in another room if you can't watch, but **don't let your emotions override what's right for the child.**

## If you choose NOT to stop the bottle
Then **brush/gargle the child after every night bottle** before sleep — otherwise the **upper jaw protrudes** and teeth suffer. (Either stop it, or commit to brushing every time.) **Always follow the sleep-training steps** alongside.
