# Bonus Q&A — No Bottle/Breast Habit, Yet Still Waking 4–5× a Night

*"I give no formula and no breastfeed at night, yet the baby still wakes 4–5 times — why?"*

## 3 major reasons + solutions
1. **Another sleep-association "help" is in play.** Bottle/breast are habits — but so are **rocking, pram-walking, car rides, and lap-carrying.** If the child falls asleep with *any* parental help, they wake every ~2 hours seeking it again. **Sleep training = falling asleep with no parental interference**, so remove *all* of these.
2. **The child is physically uncomfortable** — **gas, a tight diaper, diaper rash, or a late dinner** (undigested food), or a **disturbed gut** (no gurgling). **Fix:** shift dinner earlier, add **prebiotics + probiotics + fruits**, and at bedtime massage with **warm sesame oil + a hot pot** to calm the child.
3. **Trying to put an *active* child to sleep.** Going straight from "playing playing playing → 9 o'clock, sleep now" makes the child's brain go **alert** ("wait, I was just playing!") → more active → wakes 3–4× more. **Follow the sequence: active play → (tire out) → FIFA feed/activity → calming activity → sleep.**
