# Bonus Q&A — Elder & Younger Child Disturbing Each Other's Sleep

*"A 4-year-old (with a school routine) and a 4-month-old: I sleep-trained the baby, but when the baby wakes at night the toddler wakes too." (Applies to twins and elder/younger pairs.)*

- **Separate the children for ~10 days.** If no extra adult is available, **one child sleeps with the husband, the other with you**:
  - If the **younger** is breastfeeding-dependent, the **mother keeps the younger**; the husband takes the elder. (Or vice-versa as feasible.)
- **Why:** while the baby is still in the training phase, if both sleep together the baby learns *"if I cry, I disturb everyone"* — separation avoids that.
- **After the baby is trained**, tell **both children for the next 5 days** that they'll **sleep together again**, and do **both bedtime routines together.**
- **No extra adult / nuclear family:** take the **husband's help**, or **talk to the elder child** and accept some disturbance from the younger while you train.
- The issue should **resolve within ~7 days** (separation managed over **8–10 days**).
