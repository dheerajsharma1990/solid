# Bonus Q&A — Following the Sleep Routine, Yet Baby Still Wakes Up

*"I follow the baby's sleep routine fully, but the baby still wakes — I can't tell what's going wrong."*

Three things to check:
1. **Family interference** — is a family member undermining the method? You'll have to manage/handle that.
2. **The "no formula/no breastfeed, still waking" reasons apply here too** (see that bonus Q&A): another parental-help sleep association, physical discomfort, or trying to settle an over-active child.
3. **The nipple-to-mouth habit is likely still continuing.** If you're following the full sleep training but the baby keeps waking, it usually means the **habit isn't actually broken** yet.

**Cross-question yourself:** *what exactly are you doing each time the baby wakes at night?* You need to identify that response and **correct it** — because rewarding the wake-up (feeding/rocking/replacing the nipple) is what keeps the cycle going.
