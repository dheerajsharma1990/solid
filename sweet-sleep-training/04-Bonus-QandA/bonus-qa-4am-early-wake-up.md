# Bonus Q&A — 4 AM Wake-Up / Difficult to Settle

*"Baby is sleep-trained, sleeps 8 pm, but wakes ~4 am and becomes active." (Goal: wake any time after 6 am.)*

## 6 reasons for a 4–5 am wake-up
1. **Brain milestone** (table of 3 — 3/6/9/12 weeks, months, years).
2. **Teething** (peaks ~4 days before/after a tooth; can hit in the early morning).
3. **Overtired** — too much evening light/physical/over-stimulating play.
4. **Natural shallow sleep** in the early morning as sleep hormones drop.
5. **A sliver of light** from a corner signals "morning, get up."
6. **Hunger.**

## Solutions
- **3 strict NO's on a 4–5 am wake:** no talking (a single whisper "it's night, go back to sleep," or a pat, is okay), **no turning on the light** (or you'll have to fill 2 hours), **no eye-contact games** (engaging the brain makes resettling very hard).
- **Plan of action:** stay uninvolved, keep the room dark, resettle with patting/shushing; make the **previous dinner high-protein/high-fat** (egg white, chicken, cheese cube, cow's ghee) for longer satiety; for teething, do a **cold cold-water gum massage** before bed; if overtired, do the **acupressure/massage/sponging well**; and **remove any over-stimulating new evening activity.**
- **Crucially, don't feed/play/give milk at 4 am** (or the child learns "wake at 4 → get milk," prolonging the phase).
- The early-wake phase normally lasts **1–3 weeks** if you keep the day/first-nap/bedtime routine consistent.
