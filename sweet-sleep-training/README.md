## The course in one screen — core principles
- **Sleep is a physical need**, not emotional. A night cry is usually a **habit/physical cry** (for the nipple/help), not emotional. Do it **with respect**, with **calm/cry-immune parents** — never out of your own irritation.
- **The #1 rule:** the child must learn to **fall asleep on its own, with no parental help** (no nipple, bottle, rocking, lap, pram). Then it can be put to sleep by anyone and sleep a long stretch.
- **Fix prerequisites first** (feeding, breathing/snoring, weight, deficiency, illness) before training.
- **Environment:** pitch-dark room (8–9–10 darkness), no blue light after 8 pm, AC 23–26 °C, sleeping bag (no swaddle after 3 mo), white noise/humming.
- **Food–hormone link:** early **protein/fat dinner by 7:30** (no sweets/carbs) → low insulin → low cortisol → high **melatonin** → sleep. "More food = more sleep" is a myth.
- **Routine (same order, every night):** Feed → Hygiene → Connecting (massage/stretches/reading) → Good night (dark). Put the child down **awake.**
- **Age-wise plan:** number of naps, **wake-up windows (FIFA: <5 mo 2/2.5/2; 5 mo–1 yr 3/3.5; 1 yr+ 4/4.5/4)**, nap cut-off times, and a feed/food chart; total sleep ~10–11 hr night + day naps.
- **Methods (gentle):** **Chair/stay-in-room** (best 1 yr+) and **Leave-and-check** (faster, best <1 yr); **La Pause** for <3 mo. Avoid pick-up/put-down; cry-it-out is not taught.
- **Timeline:** night settles in **3–7 nights** (expect an **extinction-burst spike on days 5–6**); daytime naps take ~3 weeks. **Never reward crying; never break consistency for >2 days.**
- **Wean night feeds gradually** (push the gap +1 hr every 2 days, or cut duration/volume); **stop the bottle by 11 months.**
- **Bonus tools:** bedtime massage, baby yoga, smiley-ball relaxation, and **nap chat** (positive affirmations whispered in the first ~15 min of sleep, daily for 30 days).
