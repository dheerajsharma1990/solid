# Bonus 1 — Sleep Training with Teething, Travel, Miracle Weeks, Siblings & Repeats

## Teething
- Pain peaks **~4 days before and after** a tooth comes; the child is irritated, mouths everything.
- **Myth:** teething causes loose motions — false (it's the dirty objects the child chews, not teething itself; but loose motions *can* coincide).
- **Solutions:** **cold-compress gum massage** (soft cloth on finger, dipped in cold water, deep but gentle pressure — ideally ~5 min before feeding and during the bedtime massage); a **chew** (knotted denim cloth dipped in cold expressed breastmilk or cold water); a **breastmilk popsicle** (breastmilk + mango set in the fridge — recipe in MyDvija's *Yum Food Diary*); a **food teether** with the child's favorite juicy fruit (pomegranate, orange).
- Teething affects sleep for **~8 days** — **don't let it spoil the basic habits**: keep the routine to the first nap; at a **non-feed wake-up, still don't feed.**

## Travel & jet lag
- **Pre-plan mode + duration:** ideally **travel by day, arrive at night**. For long international day+night travel, **leave late evening** so the flight is dark and the child sleeps; keep the child **active during the daytime** flight hours.
- **Arriving by day:** keep the child **awake 1–2 hrs** before letting them nap. **Arriving at night:** go straight into the **dark room + normal routine.**
- **Create a sleep space** even while traveling (a shield in the back of the car; dark when it's sleep time).
- **Rules:** **no disturbance beyond ~24 hrs** (always do *some* sleep routine wherever you are — skipping it signals "not sleeping"); **keep the first nap intact**; **don't increase feeding** just because travel disturbs the child.

## Miracle / regression weeks
- Sudden frequent waking when the child is hitting a **milestone** = a **regression / "miracle" week**. Pattern = **table of 3** (3, 6, 9, 12 months, 3 years), **plus two extra common ones at ~4 and ~8 months**.
- **Don't start fresh** sleep training during one. If the child is already in it: **let go of strict discipline but keep the routine** (first nap set, dark room before 9). Trained children's regressions are **shorter** (otherwise 1–3 weeks).
- **Solutions:** consistency; try new soothing tricks **in the day**; hold off responding to crying ~12–15 min (then comfort briefly and put down awake); **don't increase night feeds.**

## Siblings / twins
- **Separate sleep spaces** until both children sleep at the same time (then they can sleep together); for **twins, use two different rooms.**
- Keep the **same routine** for both, plan the day schedule carefully, manage feed/food — **don't add night feeds** because one wakes more. Take help from the elder/expected child. Training both together takes **~7–8 days**.

## Do you have to repeat sleep training?
**Yes**, after a **drastic time-zone change**, a **geography/weather/household change** (e.g. nuclear family → a 15–20-person household), or a **disturbance lasting more than 2 days** (too many late nights/outings/relatives). **Solution:** repeat the same techniques — but for an already-trained child it takes only **1–2 days**. **Never drop consistency for more than 2 days.**
