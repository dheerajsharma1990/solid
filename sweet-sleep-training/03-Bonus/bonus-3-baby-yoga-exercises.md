# Bonus 3 — Baby Yoga: Evening/Morning Exercises

*A demonstration video (on a 14-day-old). Research-based joint movements/exercises that work the child's **fine and gross motor skills**, boost physical & motor development and **mind-body coordination**, and speed up reaching milestones — and tire the child for peaceful sleep.*

**General rule:** hold the **big joints**, move **slowly and gently** (a baby's joints are soft); do each **3–5×** if the child enjoys it, **1–2×** if the child is sleepy/pulling away; **do every move on BOTH sides** for balance.

## Hands / arms
- **Hand stretch** — gentle 1–5.
- **Up–down**, then **join–spread**, ~5×.
- **Cross** — right hand up, left hand up, crossing (kids find this fun).
- **Finger rotations** — small fingers turned slowly clockwise/anticlockwise (boosts circulation, flexibility, body control).
- **Wrist** — push firmly and slowly, up then down, 1–5 each hand.

## Legs / feet
- **Stretch** — hold big joints, gently stretch each leg, then both legs **up–down**.
- **Up & spread**, to the child's comfort, then relax.
- **Cross & press (Gomukhasana, "magical")** — cross both feet and press gently (relaxes muscles).
- **Cycle + press (gas relief)** — hold both feet, **cycle-cycle-cycle**, stretch, then fold and gently press the knees to the tummy (helps pass gas). **Don't do right after a feed** (vomiting risk).
- **Ankle/finger movements** — toe rotations clockwise/anticlockwise, ankle stretch toward you (small + big muscles all matter).

## Tummy-time neck/shoulder strengthening
- Place the baby **on the stomach, both hands forward, elbows ahead of the head**.
- Place **your right hand on the baby's right shoulder, elbow toward the left hip**, gentle pressure → the baby tries to **lift the neck**. Hold **~60 seconds**, then repeat the mirror side (left shoulder / right hip). Strengthens shoulder/neck and **speeds neck control** — the baby does the lifting itself (gentle press only).
