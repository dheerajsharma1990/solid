# Q&A — Part 1

- **"Can I sleep-train only at night?"** No. Good night sleep depends on the **daytime nap environment**, so naps must be sleep-trained too. **Night settles in ~8–10 days; daytime takes ~2–3 weeks** — be prepared.
- **"My house is very bright in the day — how do I darken the room?"** Black curtains (Amazon) + black paper / aluminium foil on the window from inside, close all artificial lights and window doors, double curtains. **No shortcut** — you *must* darken the room for every nap, however bright the house.
- **"I'm a working mother; the child stays with grandma."** Start on a **Friday** (you're home Fri–Sun to set it up); by day 3 the child is calmer anyway. Don't over-pressure grandparents — ask for the essentials ("please keep curtains down, settle them this way"); aim for **70–80%** adherence and don't stress.
- **"My child's weight is low — is sleep training safe?"** First check: is it low *to you* or *medically*? (Example: a baby born 2.7 kg, now 6 kg at 6 months — that's doubled by month 5, so **not** medically low → start immediately.) If weight has genuinely **not doubled / is underweight**, do all the preparation but keep a **minimum 2 night feeds** until weight normalizes.
- **"Is my husband's involvement necessary?"** No one's help is strictly required — you **can do it solo**. But: (1) if you take help, the helper must be **fixed** and follow the *same* method; (2) keep a **very possessive "can't-bear-the-cry" person away** for the 10 days; (3) help that **disrupts the routine** (someone slipping a feed/rocking) is worse than no help; (4) if it causes marital friction, **sleep in separate rooms for 10 days**, then resume.
- **"My child roams around the bed."** A **separate sleep space is compulsory** (Part 1). Use a **bed barrier** (Amazon) to wall off the bed, or put a mattress on the floor; the child can roam within the space, tire, and sleep. **Only if no separate space is truly possible** and you must co-sleep: **don't react/respond** — stay visible but uninvolved (pretend to sleep).
