# Q&A — Part 3

- **"Is it really necessary to watch the child cry?"** Yes — crying is part of *unlearning* (like a child crying the first few days of school/daycare): more on day 1, less on day 2, less on day 3. Do it; don't over-stress.
- **"Can I switch methods mid-way?"** Yes, 100%. The **chair method is slow**, **leave-and-check is fast** — if the child is clinging too much on the chair method, switch to leave-and-check anytime. But when you switch, **restart the day 1-2-3 / 4-5-6 schedule** of the new method.
- **"Festive season/guests ruined it — restart?"** Yes, but **don't add extra feeds** during the disruption (if your rule is no feed before 5 hrs, hold it). If you already gave more feeds, just **restart sleep training** — having done 7 days before, it now takes only **2–3 days**.
- **"How do I know my child IS sleep-trained?"** Success = the child (1) falls asleep **without the nipple, on its own** (even if via a little patting/tapping), (2) sleeps **3–4 hrs straight**, and (3) resettles with a little patting after you put it down. **Waking once or twice and needing a brief pat is NOT a failure** — even 11- and 14-year-olds wake once a night sensing a parent's touch.
- **"But the patting/2 wakings still spoil *my* sleep — can I remove that too?"** Yes, but it's the *next, harder* step: **stop the patting yourself** (the same way you removed the nipple), and over **4–5 days** the child learns to resettle without it (effectively leave-and-check / minimal response). This is **optional** — the instructor explicitly **won't guilt any mother**: if patting drains your **emotional health/irritates you, stop it** (no emotional harm to the child); if you *cherish* that connection, **keep it**.
- **Why "no nipple to sleep" is the core rule:** so the child can be put to sleep by **anyone** (husband, grandmother) and sleep a long stretch — not dependent on the mother. **Keep expectations practical.**
