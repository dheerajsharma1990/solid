# Q&A — Part 2

- **"My child cat-naps (wakes <45 min, at tiny sounds)."** There's **no direct cure** — cat-napping is a light/alert nap. It **improves a lot once the routine, dark closed room, nap timing, and night training are set**. Don't let the child fall asleep on the breast/bottle; keep them active before the nap.
- **"You didn't give the routine for an 11-month-old!"** The routines shown (6–8 mo, 8 mo, 1 yr) are **examples** — **build your own**: write the child's age and 7:30 wake, apply the **FIFA** wake-window formula (feed → activity → feed → activity → nap routine → nap), set the first nap and the gap-between-naps from the chart, and check how many naps the age needs. Don't expect spoon-feeding — make your child's plan yourself.
- **"My child still falls asleep with the nipple in his mouth."** Re-watch Part 2. Steps: (1) **shift the feed earlier** in the routine (feed → mild activity → sleep); (2) **active feeding** — no diaper/swaddle, wipe the face with warm cloth, add movement, burp on the shoulder, **keep talking** to keep the baby awake, give a **sound toy/rattle**. Falling asleep on the nipple means you're *letting* the child drift into deep sleep — and yes, removing the nipple will restart crying; **some crying is part of sleep training** (don't do it forever). The child must **unlearn "nipple = sleep"** and learn to fall asleep on its own (an inborn ability) — give it the **opportunity** for 7 days.
- **"After 7 good days: sleeps 8:30 → wakes 1 (feed) → wakes 5 crying hard → husband gives formula. Right?"** No. If the child can do **4–5 hrs without a feed** (8:30→1), then after 1 it should go to ~6. **Decide consistently:** either keep **2 night feeds at fixed 1:00 and 5:00**, or **one feed at 1:00** and **nothing before 6** (6 am+ = a day feed). **Don't feed at a drifting time** (5, then 4, then 3) — that confuses the child. Pick a fixed time.
- **"Child sleeps well but wakes every 1.5 hrs unless I rock him."** **Rocking/lap-shaking is still parental help** — sleep training means falling asleep **without** any help (not just without the breast). So wean the rocking too. (If a sleep-trained child regresses due to **teething** and you used soothing tricks, go **back to the basics** of Part 2.)
- **"Sleep-trained but still wakes once at night."** Normal — even older children wake once (heat, fan off, thirst, hunger, a dream) and resettle. **One or two brief wakings a night is fine.** Keep the rule: the child must resettle **without help** to sleep long.
- **"My breast is engorging since the child feeds less."** Mothers feeding once/twice a night won't have this. If you dropped from 5 feeds straight to 1, **reduce gradually (5→4→3→2→1)** to avoid it. For relief: **chilled cabbage leaf** in the bra (reduces supply); or **express for 1–2 days** (warm first, then express), then skip 1–2 days — supply is demand-driven and drops over **2–3 days**.
- **"Sleeps well at 8:30 but wakes at 4:30 (early waking)."** **Don't** push bedtime later (8:30–9:30 is correct). On a 4 am wake: **no interaction, no lights, no activity**; whisper "still night, mama & papa are sleeping." Re-check the **day routine and nap schedule**, look for a recent **trigger** (last 2–3 days), and **don't offer milk before 6 am** (or the child learns "wake at 4 → get milk").
